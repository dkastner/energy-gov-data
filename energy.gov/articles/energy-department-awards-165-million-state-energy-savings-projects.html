Energy Department Awards $16.5 Million for State Energy Savings Projects

August 30, 2005 - 2:53pm

Addthis

To Improve Energy Efficiency in Schools, Promote Energy-Efficient Industrial Technologies, and Support Solar, Wind and Biomass Renewable Energy Sources

WASHINGTON, DC -- Secretary of Energy Samuel W. Bodman announced today that the Department of Energy (DOE) will provide $16,509,819 for 178 energy efficiency and renewable energy projects in 42 states.  The funding for these energy-saving projects is being provided through DOE State Energy Program Special Projects competitive grants, and will be awarded in September.

State energy offices will use these funds to improve the energy efficiency of schools, homes and other buildings; promote energy-efficient industrial technologies; and support renewable energy sources such as solar, wind and biomass.  Some projects will create opportunities for distributed energy resources.

These competitive DOE awards will provide specialized funding in addition to annual formula grants given to each state energy office.                                                                

Summary of FY 2005 Awards by Category

Clean Cities (70 projects totaling $5,444,138):   Funds will support alternative fuels and alternative fuel vehicles (AFV) projects that: 1) promote AFV infrastructure development; 2) promote alternative fuel vehicles; 3) promote the acquisition of AFV school buses and refueling stations for these school buses; 4) promote the reduction and/or elimination of idling of heavy-duty vehicles and school buses; 5) support Clean Cities coalition activities; and 6) promote sustainable heavy-duty hybrid-electric vehicle fleet markets.

Industrial Technology Program (21 projects totaling $1,997,318):   These grants will help large energy-consuming industrial plants identify, quantify and implement opportunities to save energy and improve environmental performance in their facilities.

Building Codes and Standards (19 projects totaling $1,994,689):   Funds support states’ actions to adopt, update, implement, enforce and evaluate the effectiveness of their residential and commercial building energy codes.

Rebuild America   (37 projects totaling $3,528,077):  Funds support   specific and measurable energy-saving Rebuild America state program project partnerships that have defined a program and process with significant opportunity to complete building projects. DOE’s Rebuild America program is also seeking alternative means of providing products and services including:   the distribution of publications that can be used nationally; expertise and experience in building practices such as performance contracting; technical expertise in a building technology through cooperation with a university- or state- sponsored project; and a successful model for implementing building retrofit projects within a community or sector.

Building America   (5 projects totaling $478,433):   Funding supports cooperative projects involving states, the building industry, and Building America teams to conduct housing research projects and accelerate the market adoption of advanced energy-efficient residential building practices and products.

Federal Energy Management Program (3 projects totaling $444,323):   Funding supports coordinated bulk procurement of energy for federal facilities, and regional energy reliability planning and implementation.

Solar Technology Program (1 project totaling $250,000):  Innovative micro inverters are a first step toward commercialization of AC current photovoltaic (PV) products where the fully integrated PV modules/inverters provide AC power to utility-connected or mini-utility loads.   Micro inverters are small size inverters which could fit in the back of  PV modules or in the window railing of buildings.  These inverters convert DC current to an AC current signal so that appliances can utilize solar energy just as they do with conventional utility electricity.  Micro inverters must be rugged, able to withstand the brutal environments associated with rooftops and must have long lifetimes.   This project will examine the internal and external maximum temperature conditions in which micro inverters will work. 

State Wind Energy Support (5 projects totaling $278,340):   Funds will support two categories:  1) Projects to seek information on U.S. wind resources at levels above normal measurements, often capped at 60 meters.  States are requested to propose instrumentation of existing coastal and barrier island tall towers (100 meters or taller) for one year of measurements at three heights, using duplicate anemometers at each location to avoid tower shadow.  2) Projects to identify opportunities for wind applications in the treatment, movement and use of water resources.

Distributed Energy – Regional   Application   Center for Combined Cooling, Heating and Power (CHP) for Buildings and Industrial Applications (7 projects totaling $1,442,000):  The funds support established CHP Regional Application Centers currently partnering with DOE.   The objective of the Regional Application Center is to provide essential and appropriate applied research and development support, focused on the technology transfer and installation of advanced CHP technologies.  The Regional Application Center will achieve this objective through project assistance and targeted education and outreach.

Biomass (7 projects totaling $512,501): Funds will support the development of innovative state or local incentives and projects that will facilitate increased market development for bio-based power, fuels and products. 

Air Quality Integration (3 projects totaling $140,000): Funds will support states efforts to quantify, verify or validate emission reductions achieved from active projects previously funded through the State Energy Program formula grant or past Special Project activities, in a way that attempts to incorporate the emission reductions into a State Implementation Plan (SIP).

For more details on these projects contact your State Energy Office.  Contact information can be found at:
