Photo Gallery: 2015 ARPA-E Innovation Summit Technology Showcase

February 11, 2015 - 2:16pm

Addthis

1 of 10

Ford Precision Battery Tester

In order to test how long it takes a battery to decay, companies must perform years of expensive testing -- expenses that eventually get passed on to consumers. This device is able to test batteries more precisely, which cuts testing time and will ultimately lower the price of the car.

Image: Photo courtesy of Matty Greene, Energy Department.

2 of 10

Fuel Cells in Space

This is a model of a lighter and less complicated fuel cell with no moving parts that NASA has developed to fuel the next generation of spacecraft.

Image: Photo courtesy of Matty Greene, Energy Department.

3 of 10

A Replacement for Trailered Generators

This Medium Tactical Vehicle Replacement or MTVR is a truck that has the ability to serve as a mobile radar or mobile command center without the need for additional support vehicles or trailered generators.

Image: Photo courtesy of Matty Greene, Energy Department.

4 of 10

ThermoLift: Efficient Natural Gas Heating & Cooling

ThermoLift, a recipient of a grant from the Energy Department’s Buildings Technology Office, uses thermal energy from natural gas to heat and cool efficiently -- reducing energy costs by up to 50 percent.

Image: Photo courtesy of Matty Greene, Energy Department.

5 of 10

The Difference Between Electric Vehicle Batteries

A presenter breaks down how batteries in various electric vehicles differ and how that affects their performance.

Image: Photo courtesy of Matty Greene, Energy Department.

6 of 10

Local Motors' 3D-printed Car

The Local Motors 3D-printed car -- the first-ever 3D-printed vehicle -- was a major attraction at the showcase. The design was the result of a contest with over 200 submissions.

Image: Photo courtesy of Matty Greene, Energy Department.

7 of 10

Customize your Car

Instead of the thousands of parts that make up the average car, the 3D-printed vehicle is made up of fewer than 50 parts. One day you might be able to design your car, print it, add a motor and go!

Image: Photo courtesy of Matty Greene, Energy Department.

8 of 10

Simple Problem, Complex Solution

The safest way to transport compressed natural gas, or CNG, is in a cylindrical canister, but these cylinders are bulky and take up lots of room in vehicles. This new design allows for safe containment of CNG while allowing for easier storage.

Image: Photo courtesy of Matty Greene, Energy Department.

9 of 10

Sorting Metals

After a car or appliance is shredded, lots of small pieces of metal end up going to the landfill because there isn’t a way to sort them out from the other scrap. This machine uses magnetic fields at varying frequencies to repel different metals and sort aluminum from copper from scrap rubber and plastic -- allowing companies to sell the metal at higher rates and keeping it from being thrown away.

Image: Photo courtesy of Matty Greene, Energy Department.

10 of 10

Saving Energy by Repelling Water

Frost on aluminum refrigerator coils builds up and begins to block the airway, meaning refrigerators have to defrost four to six times per day -- a major energy cost. The aluminum pictured on the left is coated with Slips, a coating that keeps water from sticking to the surface, allowing refrigerator coils to defrost only once per week -- saving both money and energy.

Image: Photo courtesy of Matty Greene, Energy Department.

2015 ARPA-E Innovation Summit

  • Join the conversation by using the #ARPAE15 hashtag on social media.
  • Check out this photo gallery from the 2014 Technology Showcase.
  • Stay tuned for more updates!

Each year the Technology Showcase at the ARPA-E Innovation Summit presents cutting-edge energy technologies, and this year was no different. From a heating and cooling system that works on thermal energy powered by natural gas to the first ever 3D-printed car, breakthrough technologies were on display around every corner. Along with these next-generation technologies, experts were on hand to answer questions about how things work and when these new products and systems will be available to the public.

Check out the photo gallery above to learn about some of the amazing technological advances on display at the showcase.

If you’d like to find out more about ARPA-E or the Summit, visit arpa-e.energy.gov.
