Slideshow: Ready, Set, NASCAR Green at Richmond International Raceway

April 28, 2014 - 3:54pm

Addthis

1 of 9

Secretary Ernest Moniz displays the green flag prior to the NASCAR race at the Richmond International Raceway. | Photo courtesy of Jeff Zelevansky, NASCAR via Getty Images.

2 of 9

NASCAR drivers use some of the most advanced fuel technologies, helping educate fans about new technology. Before every race, the cars must pass inspection to ensure they meet NASCAR standards. Pictured here is part of the inspection process at the Richmond International Raceway. | Photo by Rebecca Matulka, Energy Department.

3 of 9

Secretary Ernest Moniz hears from Dr. Mike Lynch, vice president of Green Innovation at NASCAR, about NASCAR's efforts to reduce the sport's environmental impact. | Photo courtesy of NASCAR via Getty Images.

4 of 9

As part of its green efforts, NASCAR has partnered with Elgin Sweeper to save jet fuel and reduce NASCAR's carbon footprint. The Elgin Sweeper is used to help clean and dry tracks during a race's caution period. | Photo by Rebecca Matulka, Energy Department.

5 of 9

Tires are an important part of every NASCAR event with each driver using multiple sets of tires a race. To reduce their impact on the sport, Goodyear Tire -- the official tire supplier of NASCAR -- recycles 120,000 tires every year. | Photo by Rebecca Matulka, Energy Department.

6 of 9

Secretary Ernest Moniz accepts an honorary plaque from the President of Richmond International Raceway Dennis Bickmeier during the pre-race ceremonies. | Photo courtesy of Brian Lawdermilk, NASCAR via Getty Images.

7 of 9

Secretary Ernest Moniz waves the green flag, signaling the start of the NASCAR Sprint Cup Series Toyota Owners 400 at Richmond International Raceway. | Photo courtesy of Brian Lawdermilk, NASCAR via Getty Images.

8 of 9

Since 2011, NASCAR has used E15 -- a biofuel blended with 15 percent American ethanol -- to fuel the cars. NASCAR has completed nearly 6 million miles using E15 and racing teams report an increase of up to 10+ horsepower. | Photo by Rebecca Matulka, Energy Department.

9 of 9

Secretary Moniz visits NASCAR driver No. 16, Greg Biffle's, pit box during the race and listens to the conversation between Biffle and his pit crew on the scanner. Pit crews rely heavily on math and engineering skills to ensure the cars run as efficiently as possible. | Photo by Rebecca Matulka, Energy Department.

Rebecca Matulka
Former Digital Communications Specialist, Office of Public Affairs

Last weekend, Energy Secretary Ernest Moniz waved the iconic green flag -- kicking off the NASCAR race at the Richmond International Raceway.

While at the track, Secretary Moniz learned firsthand how NASCAR is working to reduce the sportâs environmental impact and educating its fans to do the same -- from using advanced biofuels and testing fuel cell generators to creating an extensive recycling program and incorporating renewable energy into the races. To continue to drive its environmental efforts forward, NASCAR and the Energy Department signed a Memorandum of Understanding last September, outlining ways to support the deployment of transformative clean energy technologies that lower greenhouse gas emissions.
