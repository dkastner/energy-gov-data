3 Reasons to Attend the 2016 Better Buildings Summit HINT: Industry Experts, Networking, Recognition

April 13, 2016 - 5:13pm

Addthis

1 of 8

Last year, Dr. David Danielson, Assistant Secretary for Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy, presented a keynote address at a recognition ceremony. Register for the 2016 Summit to learn how to leverage no-cost DOE tools to achieve significant energy savings and explore future opportunities to advance energy efficiency in your organization.

2 of 8

Jay Wrobel, Manager of Technical Assistance at the DOE Advanced Manufacturing Office, moderated a panel on commercial and industrial energy efficiency programs at the 2015 Summit. This year’s summit will feature speakers from the commercial, industrial, public, and multifamily sectors that will share how they draw on energy efficiency technologies, business practices, and partnerships to save money on utility bills, create new jobs, and improve their organization’s competitiveness.

3 of 8

Mark Johnson, Director of the Advanced Manufacturing Office (AMO) congratulated a recipient at a recognition ceremony for Superior Energy Performance® (SEP™) certified facilities at last year’s Summit.

4 of 8

Last year, attendees listened to remarks from leading organizations and experts at the opening day keynote session. The 2016 Better Buildings Summit will feature speakers from the commercial, industrial, public, and multifamily sectors that will share how they draw on energy efficiency technologies, business practices, and partnerships to save money on utility bills, create new jobs, and improve their organization’s competitiveness.

5 of 8

Attendees from the 2015 Summit listened to experts and discussed future trends and the next generation of opportunities at a rapt workshop. This year, attendees can join others to grow their network and make connections with industry peers, technical experts, and other Better Buildings partners and allies.

6 of 8

Attendees from the 2015 Summit networked with Better Buildings partners and shared innovative, replicable solutions at a networking reception. The Better Buildings Summit is an great opportunity to interact with attendees from multiple sectors.

7 of 8

Challenge Partners, from the 2015 Better Buildings Summit, were recognized at a special event in the Indian Treaty Room in the White House campus.

8 of 8

At the 2015 Summit, more than 900 participants engaged in dialogue focused on sharing proven approaches. This year, attendees can expect to join more summit partners and stakeholders to learn the latest in efficiency and how to make energy efficiency a smart business decision for your organization.

Space is still available for the 2016 Better Buildings Summit, taking place May 9-11 in Washington, DC. This year’s Summit features a range of opportunities for manufacturers to gain new technical knowledge in energy systems operations, learn the latest energy-saving best practices and technologies, and network with peers on successful Better Plants and Better Buildings solutions. Register for the Summit today so you can:

Hear from DOE experts: Leading research and development experts from DOE’s Advanced Manufacturing Office (AMO) will join you on Monday for the first ever Better Plants Technology Transfer lunch. These experts are there for you to ask questions or to share their insights for different industrial sectors on new technologies, innovations, and issues that affect energy productivity in the manufacturing sector, including: advanced sensors and controls; combined heat and power; 3D printing; process intensification and water-energy impacts. AMO’s management team as well as Director, Mark Johnson, will also be on hand to discuss the Department’s energy priorities as well as answer your energy and manufacturing questions.

In addition, a new feature at the 2016 Summit is the “Ask-an-Expert” booth, staffed by researchers from DOE national labs. These experts will be available to hold one-on-one and small group discussions Tuesday and Wednesday covering such topics as energy management, water efficiency, pumping systems, compressed air, and industrial assessments.

Also, on Monday after the Technology Transfer Lunch, don’t miss a special “armchair discussion” between AMO Director Mark Johnson and ACEEE Senior Director for Research Neal Elliott on big picture trends affecting manufacturers and energy now and into the future. Mark and Neal will share their thoughts on the current state of U.S. manufacturing, energy and commodities price projections, and how the information technology revolution and smart manufacturing are impacting energy productivity in the manufacturing sector.

Learn from your peers: Like past years’, the 2016 Summit features over 100 sessions broken out into sector-specific and cross-cutting tracks. In the industrial track, representatives from companies including Cummins, Toyota, General Mills, Texas Instruments, and Volvo will present technical details on successful projects that helped save energy and water in their plants.

Manufacturers are well represented on cross-cutting panels as well. Procter & Gamble will describe a unique employee engagement program it implemented across the company; Johnson & Johnson will present on its efforts to build and maintain senior management support for its energy and sustainability program, which features a multi-million dollar capital fund for CO2 reduction projects; Eastman Chemical will detail its water strategy and how it was informed by and complements the company’s energy strategy.

Get recognized for your success: The Summit is a great opportunity to celebrate success across the Better Buildings partnership! DOE will be highlighting results from the Better Buildings Challenge in a press release, progress report, and through social media. New Better Plants Program partners, goal achievers, and companies with plants certified under DOE’s Superior Energy Performance Program will be recognized at a special Monday ceremony.

For more details, check out the full program here. The Better Buildings Summit will take place over two-and-a-half days, with more than 200 speakers will share best practices, lessons learned, and successful models. We hope to see you there!
