AMO Innovations Draw Crowds at Hannover Messe 2016

May 10, 2016 - 5:56pm

Addthis

The NNMI booth at Hannover Messe 2016. Photo courtesy of PowerAmerica.

During the week of April 25-29, over 190,000 visitors took over the city of Hanover, Germany for the 70th annual Hannover Messe, the world’s largest industrial conference. Throughout the week, world industry leaders attended applications-oriented forums, talks, panel discussions and keynote speakers and shared their wealth of knowledge on the issues facing industry. Over 50 percent of all global primary energy consumed is for industrial use, so Hannover Messe serves as a ‘hot spot’ to discuss and introduce new technologies and practices that cut energy use and implement clean energy technologies. Hannover Messe’s showcase and topic areas such as industrial automation, energy, industrial supply, research and technology, young-tech enterprises, and workforce development line up with the Advanced Manufacturing Office’s (AMO) key research, development, and deployment activities. With both a booth display and a speaking engagement from AMO Director Mark Johnson, the Department of Energy (DOE) had a forum to engage with and educate attendees about AMO’s work and partnership opportunities.

During his lecture as part of Hannover Messe’s Tech Transfer – Gateway2Innovation forum, Dr. Johnson pointed to the National Network of Manufacturing Innovation (NNMI) as a way to provide access to cutting-edge capabilities and equipment, foster collaboration between industry and academia, and educate and train the workforce in new technologies. He gave two examples of real, tangible results of American ingenuity that were on display at Hannover Messe: the 3D printed Shelby Cobra and the Fast Charger. These displays, which came from IACMI and PowerAmerica, respectively, show the potential for AMO’s strategic investments to bring ideas to commercialization. The Cobra and Fast Charger were a huge hit at Hannover Messe, attracting everyone from students to dignitaries. President Obama’s opening remarks included a shoutout to the Cobra (timestamp 3:12) and Jay Timmons, President and CEO of the National Association of Manufacturers, stopped by to take a few pictures in the driver’s seat.

Hannover Messe was a great opportunity not only to educate the world about the work of the NNMI, but also to identify and engage with potential partners who could join the more than 800 companies, universities, and non-profit members of the NNMI. This collaborative effort is critical to reduce the cost and risk of commercializing new platform technologies and develop innovative methodologies and practices for supply-chain integration, which is why DOE leads two NNMI institutes.

  • PowerAmerica is accelerating the adoption of advanced semiconductor components made with silicon carbide (SiC) and gallium nitride (GaN) into a wide range of products and systems.
  • The Institute for Advanced Composites Manufacturing Innovation (IACMI) is catalyzing the development and adoption of cutting edge manufacturing technologies for low-cost, energy-efficient manufacturing of advanced polymer composites for vehicles, wind turbines, and compressed gas storage.

The NNMI is continuing to expand, identifying new Institute topics focused on improving the competitiveness of American manufacturing. Two new DOE-sponsored Institutes are currently in the works:

  • Funding was announced for the Smart Manufacturing Institute in September of last year to support research and development advancements that can reduce the cost of deployment for technologies such as advanced sensors, controls, platforms, and modeling for manufacturing. The selection announcement is anticipated soon.
  • Last week, DOE announced $70 million in funding over five years for the Modular Chemical Process Intensification Institute, which will be the fourth institute supported by AMO. It will focus on developing breakthrough technologies to significantly boost the energy efficiency of manufacturing chemicals used across an array of U.S. industries, from chemicals manufacturers to producers of biofuels.

To learn more about the NNMI as it continues to grow, visit manufacturing.gov.
