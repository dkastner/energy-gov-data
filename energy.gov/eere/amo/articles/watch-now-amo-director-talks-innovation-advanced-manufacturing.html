WATCH NOW: AMO Director Discusses Evolution and Future of Manufacturing

AMO Director Mark Johnson recently discussed the evolution of manufacturing and the future of innovation at the Office of Energy Efficiency & Renewable Energy’s EnergyTalk. He was joined by Former Acting Assistant Secretary, Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy, David Friedman; Principal Deputy Assistant Secretary, Fossil Energy, Doug Hollett; and Senior Advisor for Strategic Projects, Office of Technology Transitions, Don McDonald.

Click here to watch the EnergyTalk broadcast.

WEBINAR: Combined Heat and Power Technical Assistance Partnerships – January 26

The Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy’s Advanced Manufacturing Office, will host an informational webinar on January 26 at 3:00 PM ET regarding the Funding Opportunity Announcement (FOA) entitled “Combined Heat and Power Technical Assistance Partnerships.” No new information will be presented during the webinar and EERE will not answer any attendee questions.

Register for the webinar here.

For more information, view the full solicitation.

DEADLINE: Advanced Manufacturing Projects for Emerging Research Exploration Letter of Intent – January 31

Letters of intent for the Funding Opportunity Announcement (FOA) entitled “Advanced Manufacturing Projects for Emerging Research Exploration” are due January 31, 2017 at 5:00 PM ET. Submission deadline for full applications is March 30, 2017 at 5:00 PM ET.

For more information, view the full solicitation.

DEADLINE: AMO’s Multi-Year Program Plan Draft Available for Public Feedback and Comments – February 10

The Advanced Manufacturing Office (AMO) invites you to review the Multi-Year Program Plan and send your feedback and comments to AMO_MYPPInfo@ee.doe.gov by February 10, 2017.

AMO’s draft Multi-Year Program Plan (MYPP) sets forth the mission, goals, and plan of the Office. This MYPP is intended for use as an operational guide to help AMO manage and coordinate its activities, as well as a resource to help communicate its priorities and opportunities to stakeholders and the public by identifying the technology, outreach, and crosscutting activities the Office plans to focus on over the next five years.

ARPA-E Energy Innovation Summit to Feature Cutting-Edge Technologies and Nation’s Energy Innovators – February 27–March 1

The Department of Energy’s Advanced Research Projects Agency-Energy (ARPA-E) will host its eight annual Energy Innovation Summit from February 27, 2017–March 1, 2017 in National Harbor, Maryland. More than 2,200 participates are expected to attend and discuss cutting-edge energy issues and cultivate relationships with leaders from industry, government and academia to advance technologies towards deployment.

More information regarding the Summit can be found here.

DEADLINE APPROACHING: HPC4Mfg Industry Engagement Day – March 2-3, 2017

Do not miss your opportunity to attend the HPC4Manufacturing Industry Engagement Day “Spurring Innovation in U.S. Manufacturing Through Advanced Computing.” The event will connect world-class experts to reflect on the global trends of High Performance Computing (HPC) in manufacturing. Opportunities for access to capabilities that will allow for adoption of HPC will be presented, with opportunities for direct one-on-one engagement to discuss your specific needs and interests.

The deadline for online registration is Tuesday, January 31, 2017.

Register today!

Energy Department Introduces New Financing Navigator for Energy Efficiency Projects

The U.S. Department of Energy’s Better Buildings Initiative introduced a new, web-based financing navigator to help private and public sector organizations discover financing solutions for energy efficiency projects that meet their unique needs. Through the Better Buildings Financing Navigator, building owners, facility and energy managers, and other decision-makers can connect with financiers, including banks and financial institutions, to pursue energy-saving measures.

Click here to begin searching for ways to finance your energy efficiency projects.

DOE Releases Enhanced Database Tool for Renewables and Energy Efficiency Incentives

Operated by the N.C. Clean Energy Technology Center at NC State University and funded by the Department of Energy, the Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency® (DSIRE) is a comprehensive source of information on incentives and policies that support renewables and energy efficiency in the United States.

This interactive tool can be used to find customized policies and incentive programs for energy users to capitalize on, on the local, state, and regional level due to the integration from the State Incentives and Resources database (SIR).

Click here to learn more about the tool and to find incentives near you.
