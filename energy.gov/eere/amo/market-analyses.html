Market Analyses

Need information on the market potential for combined heat and power (CHP) in the U.S.? These assessments and analyses cover a wide range of markets including commercial and institutional buildings and facilities, district energy, and industrial sites. The market potential for CHP at federal sites and in selected states/regions is also examined.

Commercial
  • CHP and Bioenergy Systems for Landfills and Wastewater Treatment Plants Part I, 17 pp and Part II, 28 pp, Nov. 2007
  • Cooling, Heating, and Power for Commercial Buildings: Benefits Analysis, 310 pp, April 2002
  • Engine Driven Combined Heat and Power: Arrow Linen Supply, 21 pp, Dec. 2008
  • Integrated Energy Systems for Buildings: A Market Assessment, 77 pp, Sept. 2002
  • Market Assessment of CHP for New Commercial Buildings and Critical Infrastructure Facilities, 35 pp, Sept. 2006
  • Market Potential for Advanced Thermally Activated Building Combined Heat and Power (BCHP) in Five National Account Sectors, 49 pp, May 2003
  • Opportunities for Combined Heat and Power at Wastewater Treatment Facilities: Market Analysis and Lessons from the Field, 57 pp, Oct. 2011
  • Opportunities for Combined Heat and Power in Data Centers, 64 pp, March 2009
  • Targeted CHP Outreach in Selected Sectors of the Commercial Market, 161 pp, 2004
District Energy/Institutional
  • Cool Trends in District Energy, 4 pp, June 2005
  • Cool Trends on Campus, 8 pp, May 2005
  • Cooling, Heating, and Power in the Nation's Colleges and Universities: Census, Survey, and Lessons Learned, 37 pp, Feb. 2002
  • Current CHP Opportunities at U.S. Colleges and Universities, 21 pp, Nov. 2003
  • District Energy in Cities, 71 pp, March 2015

  • Opportunities for Micropower and Fuel Cell/Gas Turbine Hybrid Systems in Industrial Applications, 180 pp and report appendix, 59 pp, Jan. 2000
  • Sector Profiles of Significant Large CHP Markets, 49 pp, Mar. 2004
Multifamily Housing
