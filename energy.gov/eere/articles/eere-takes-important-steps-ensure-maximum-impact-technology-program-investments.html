EERE Takes Important Steps to Ensure Maximum Impact of Technology Program Investments

November 20, 2014 - 6:06pm

Addthis

Tracking impact of EERE’s investments in wind, solar and other programs is essential to achieve maximum return for taxpayer investment. | Photos courtesy of the National Renewable Energy Laboratory

John Lushetsky
Chief Operating Officer Office of Energy Policy and Systems Analysis

The Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE) is at the center of creating the clean energy economy today, developing and delivering market-driven solutions for energy-saving homes, buildings, and manufacturing; sustainable transportation; and renewable electricity generation.  Such a large and critical goal requires EERE to leverage a number of management techniques and tools to ensure taxpayer-funded investments are directed to achieve the intended high value impact. One such tool is to perform “retrospective impact analysis,” effectively looking backwards over time and assessing how EERE has accelerated development and commercialization of technologies and produced a return on public investment that contributes to the nation’s economic growth.

Retrospective impact analyses – a subset of which is referred to as “counterfactual” studies – are grounded in the fields of economics and public policy and have been successfully applied by other government organizations such as the National Institute of Standards and Technology within the Department of Commerce as well as the U.S. Department of Agriculture. EERE has previously completed several impact assessments on different programs. For example, a 2013 analysis determined that the Energy Department’s $971 million R&D investment in advanced battery technology for electric drive vehicles (EDVs) resulted in to the commercialization of the 2.4 million EDVs sold between 1999-2012 that are projected to reduce U.S. fuel consumption by $16.7 billion through 2020.  The study also found that research funded by EERE’s Vehicle Technologies Office contributed to 112 patents in energy storage from 1976 to 2012 and is ranked first in patent citations among the top-ten companies.

Another study of the Solar Energy Technologies Program completed in 2010 showed that EERE’s investment in solar photovoltaic R&D accelerated the reduction in PV module production cost by an estimated 12 years, and created over a $15 billion return to tax payers in the form of economic activity within the U.S.

EERE has recently taken significant steps to increase the use of retrospective impact analysis within its programs and build on past successes.  In September and October, staff across EERE participated in 3 days of program evaluation training. Participants were taught about the necessary tools to plan and manage evaluation projects and how to use the results to support budgeting, planning, improvement, and communication of the programs they manage. The training was developed and led by EERE’s Jeff Dowd and Yaw Agyeman from Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL), both of whom have developed and supervised impact analysis for EERE before, and also leveraged recognized outside experts in the field of program evaluation. 

More than 45 EERE staff involved in managing evaluation-related activities within technology programs participated in parts or all of the training courses. Training was offered at the Department of Energy offices in Washington, D.C., as well as Golden, Colorado. Classes were at capacity, with staff working together on group exercises that put their newly honed skills to the test.

Along with this training, EERE also recently released updates to its guide to performing impact evaluation assessment of R&D investments. The guide, originally issued in 2009, was peer reviewed by experts in the field and provides the most up-to-date methods and approaches for how third-party evaluators should conduct impact evaluation studies for EERE in a consistent way.

The management of taxpayer dollars is a responsibility for all EERE staff. Our scientists, technical experts, project managers, and other specialists are passionate about their roles in the future of clean energy economy and the successes of the projects and programs they manage. The focused training and the updated guide gives EERE important tools to ensure we can complete our mission in the most efficient and impactful way possible.
