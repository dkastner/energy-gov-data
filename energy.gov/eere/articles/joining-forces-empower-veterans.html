Joining Forces to Empower Veterans

November 10, 2015 - 11:20am

Addthis

Sarah Wagoner
Communications Specialist, Wind and Water Power Technologies Office

Through the Energy Department’s partnership with the federal Joining Forces campaign, companies from the energy, manufacturing, transportation, and information technology sectors have committed to hiring approximately 90,000 veterans and military spouses through 2020. In an effort to meet this goal, the Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE) works with partners to connect skilled veterans with careers in the clean energy sector.

Showing our support, EERE and partners are taking steps to further solve America’s energy challenges and advance wind energy and hydropower industries by leveraging the skill sets of our military veterans. Organizations such as the National Hydropower Association (NHA) and the American Wind Energy Association (AWEA) encourage its member companies to hire and train veterans.

As the United States makes major investments in clean, renewable energy, the need for a well-trained workforce is critical. One of NHA’s members, MWH Global, Inc. (MWH), a strategic consulting, technical engineering, and construction services firm, is committed to transitioning service members through wellness, education, and employment opportunities. MWH connects veterans to valued, visible, and challenging projects, along with on-the-job training and mentorship. As of the end of September, the firm hired 20 new veterans and pledged to fill 250 positions over the next five years.

Employment in the U.S. wind industry has the potential to reach up to 600,000 jobs by mid-century according to the Energy Department’s Wind Vision report. Industry jobs may require familiarity with heavy equipment, mechanical and technical skills, and the ability to work independently, which for many veterans is a good fit with their skills and knowledge base.

AWEA, the national trade association for the U.S. wind energy industry, is encouraging its member companies to hire 10% veterans and military spouses for the wind energy workforce over the next five years. Their efforts to further support the Joining Forces initiative include promoting job openings on the Veterans Employment Center, establishing an internal veterans’ affinity group­—a subcommittee that evaluates and develops programs for hiring veterans—and providing hiring professionals with training to better understand military skills and culture.

The skills, experience, and dedication of America’s veterans and military spouses strengthen the wind energy and hydropower industries. By unlocking these opportunities and other resources available to them, former military members are making significant contributions to our nation’s clean energy future.  And their ranks are growing. For more on wind energy education and training programs, check out our WINDExchange education page.
