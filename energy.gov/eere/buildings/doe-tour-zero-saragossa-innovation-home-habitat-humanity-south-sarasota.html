DOE Tour of Zero: The Saragossa Innovation Home by Habitat for Humanity, South Sarasota

1 of 13

Habitat for Humanity South Sarasota built the Saragossa Innovation Home in Venice, Florida, to the DOE Zero Energy Ready Home (ZERH) program requirements.

2 of 13

This affordable 1,290-square-foot home will cost its homeowners less than $70 per month in energy bills.

3 of 13

The home’s interior and exterior lighting includes advanced-technology LEDs and high-efficiency fluorescent lights.

4 of 13

The home meets the Florida Water Star guidelines with water-saving faucets, toilets, and showerheads.

5 of 13

ENERGY STAR ceiling fans provide cooling air movement so residents may be able to use less air conditioning.

6 of 13

Energy-efficient appliances like the ENERGY STAR certified refrigerator and dishwasher add to utility bill savings.

7 of 13

Zero-VOC interior paints, ceramic tile instead of carpet, bathroom exhaust fans, and construction details that keep moisture out of walls are among the features that helped this home earn an EPA Indoor airPLUS certification.

8 of 13

The Saragossa Innovation Home’s walls are constructed with foam-filled concrete blocks then insulated along the interior face with ¾-inch extruded polystyrene foam insulation board and a perforated foil insulation sheet for a total insulation value of R-17.5.

9 of 13

This sealed attic has 5.5 inches (R-20) of spray foam insulation along the underside of the roof deck providing a conditioned attic space for the heating and cooling ducts.

10 of 13

A high-efficiency 15.5 SEER ducted mini-split heat pump located in the unvented, conditioned attic provides heating and cooling.

11 of 13

Quiet ENERGY STAR-rated exhaust fans provide spot ventilation and are integrated with the home’s fresh air ventilation system.

12 of 13

An outside air duct brings in fresh air that is filtered and distributed to the home through the heat pump’s air handler and ducts. The system is electronically controlled in conjunction with the home’s exhaust fans for balanced ventilation.

13 of 13

All Habitat for Humanity South Sarasota homes are built with borate-treated wood framing to discourage termites and other pests from compromising the structure of their Florida homes.

“It’s only two degrees warmer in the attic than it is down here. That is amazing to me.”

“It’s only two degrees warmer in the attic than it is down here. That is amazing to me. I just can't understand how that works so well in Florida because the roof is usually so hot.”

-- Homeowner

 

Key Features

• High-performance insulation system for enhanced quiet and comfort
• Comprehensive draft protection
• Fresh air system for cleaner, healthier indoor air
• High-efficiency comfort system
• High-efficiency appliances and advanced lighting technology for energy and water savings
