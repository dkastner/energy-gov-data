What is the Technology-to-Market Initiative?

BTO’s Technology-to-Market (T2M) team drives high impact technologies from R&D to market readiness, preparing these technologies for real building demonstration, market deployment, and ultimately mass-market adoption.

Many technologies developed in the lab never make it to the commercial world, failing to make it through the virtual chasm that separates applied research from technology demonstration. As seed funding for research declines, revenues from commercialization may not appear in time for the technology to reach the market.

The T2M Initiative helps High Impact Technologies bridge the gap between technology R&D and technology commercialization. Activities such as Technology Challenges, laboratory or test bed demonstrations, and commercialization plan support all provide the boost needed for technologies to survive this critical stage of development. BTO’s Core National Labs play a critical role in working with private companies to bring energy efficient technologies to market.

BTO Tech-to-Market Projects

Projects in BTO's tech-to-market portfolio address challenges met throughout the spectrum of technology development, adoption and market transformation; bridging gaps between manufacturers, distribution partners and end-users to streamline and accelerate the pathway to market adoption. Each project encompasses cross-cutting work between the Emerging Technologies (ET) program and the Commercial and Residential Buildings Integration (CBI/RBI) programs:

  • Air and Water-Resistive Barrier Technologies - Oak Ridge National Laboratory and 3M
  • Sensor Suitcase - Pacific Northwest National Laboratory and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory
  • VOLTTRON - Pacific Northwest National Laboratory and Transformative Wave
BTO Tech-to-Market Initiatives

Resources to help design, scale up, and commercialize emerging technologies.

  • JUMP: Crowdsourcing competition focused on bringing innovative technology design ideas to market through a set of campaigns with industry partners.
  • BTO Catalyst: Crowdsourcing competition seeking innovative software solutions to better engage building occupants and improve energy management in commercial buildings.
  • BTO Lab-Corps Teams: BTO is working with four national labs under the DOE Lab-Corps pilot program to help advance the commercialization process.
