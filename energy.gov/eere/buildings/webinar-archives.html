Advanced Envelope Research for Factory Built Housing: December 14, 2011

This webinar, presented by research team ARIES Collaborative, discussed the Advanced Envelope Research project, which aims to develop new methods of factory-built high performance thermal envelope systems. The webinar reported on interim findings including: selection of materials; potential for reducing energy use; ability to moderate and control moisture levels within the wall; compliance with the building codes and standards; ease of integration into the factory production process; and, cost of implementation.

View the presentation slides and webinar recording (WMV 20 MB). 

Back to Top

Hybrid Foundation Insulation Retrofits: November 30, 2011

This webinar, presented by research team Building Science Corporation, discussed insulating foundations and controlling water leakage as a critical measure for reducing heating load in homes in cold climates. This overview of "hybrid" type of insulation and water control interior retrofit to foundations included discussion of the physics of insulating below-grade walls, potential durability risks associated with these measures, material selection, and code issues.

View the presentation slides and webinar recording (WMV 20 MB).

An Intro to the Cool Energy House Retrofit Demonstration Project: November 14, 2011

This webinar presented a demonstration project in progress by the Building America Research Alliance (BARA) and the Consortium for Advanced Residential Buildings (CARB). The Cool Energy House will be educational demonstration home in Windermere, Florida. Builders, remodelers, and the general public will have an opportunity to tour the home and learn about deep-energy remodeling during the 2012 International Builders show. The webinar provided attendees with a tour of the cost/benefit approach the project is taking to build healthy, comfortable, and energy-efficient homes.

View the presentation slides and webinar recording (video). 

Back to Top

Opportunities to Apply Phase Change Materials to Building Enclosures: November 11, 2011

This webinar, presented by research team Fraunhofer Center for Sustainable Energy Systems (CSE), reviewed basic physical characteristics and thermal properties of phase change materials (PCMs) and provided guidance on how to effectively apply PCMs in buildings in the United States. PCMs as building materials components have the potential to achieve significant reductions in space cooling energy consumption and peak cooling loads.

View the presentation slides and webinar recording (WMV 21 MB). 

Back to Top

Ductless Hydronic Distribution Systems: November 8, 2011

This webinar was presented by research team Alliance for Residential Building Innovation (ARBI), and reviewed findings from a feasibility study of ductless hydronic distribution systems in new homes and deep retrofits. The potential to eliminate ducting in homes using hydronic heating and cooling holds promise for energy savings, especially for new high performance buildings and deep retrofits with low heating and cooling loads. Presenters discussed the advantages of hydronic distribution over traditional forced air systems.

View the presentation slides and webinar recording (WMV 15 MB). 

Back to Top

Ventilation in Multifamily Buildings: November 1, 2011

This webinar was presented by research team Consortium for Advanced Residential Buildings (CARB), and discussed ventilation strategies for multifamily buildings, including how to successfully implement those strategies through smart design, specification, and construction techniques. Presented from a building science perspective, common shortcomings in conventional design practices, and pros and cons of different mechanical system solutions, and solutions for existing buildings that currently have no ventilation were discussed.

View the presentation slides and webinar recording (WMV 23 MB). 

Back to Top

Dual Integrated Appliances as an Energy and Safety Solution for Low-Income Weatherization: October 19, 2011

This webinar was presented by research team NorthernSTAR Building America Partnership, and provided technical information from Building America research on dual integrated appliances (DIA) or combination systems. Presenters discussed how they work, the benefits of these systems for low-income weatherization programs and homeowners, how to size a DIA system, and areas of potential improvement in the design and installation.

View the presentation slides and webinar recording (video).

Sealing of Home Enclosures with Aerosol Particles: October 14, 2011

This webinar was presented by research team Building Industry Research Alliance (BIRA), and provided information about a project that uses existing aerosol duct sealing technology to seal the entire building enclosure in order to achieve greater airtightness and energy and cost savings.

View the presentation slides and webinar recording (WMV 12 MB).

Energy Star Hot Water Systems for High Performance Homes: September 30, 2011

This webinar was presented by research team Building America Partnership for Improved Residential Construction (BA-PIRC), and provided information about how to achieve energy savings from solar water heating, electric dedicated heat pump water heating, and gas tankless systems.

Building Energy Optimization (BEopt) Software Tool: November 2010

This two-part webinar series provided an overview of the BEopt tool, which is designed to identify optimally efficient designs for new and existing homes at the lowest possible cost:

  • Part 1 focuses on the basics of using BEopt in analyzing buildings for new construction applications, as well as reviews of inputs, outputs, and the library manager. View the webinar (WMV 50 MB).
  • Part 2 outlines the retrofit analysis and optimization capabilities of BEopt. View the webinar (WMV 49 MB).
