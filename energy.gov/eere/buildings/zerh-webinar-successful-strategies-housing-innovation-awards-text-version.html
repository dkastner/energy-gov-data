Joe Nebbia:
I would say that the best advantage to submitting more than one home is, again, when you have different approaches or very different style of homes to submit. So let's say you're a custom builder and you have sort of a large high-end custom home, and then you have maybe a different style more-modest custom home. If they're different enough in style, it does make sense to submit both. If you've got a townhouse row and you've also got single-family homes, maybe you could submit the townhouses -- that project under multifamily, while doing your single-family under production, for example. And then sometimes a builder will have market-ready and affordable, and certainly submit both there. If you've got multiple homes in the same category, it just depends on how different they are, whether it's worth -- whether there's an advantage in submitting them. Again, if you've got three of the same model, there may not be an extreme advantage of submitting that. In the Tour of Zero; they're all going to look basically the same. But if they're significantly different, then that could get you some extra PR. As far as winning an award or a grand award, again, if you've got three very similar homes, or four or five very similar homes, there does not appear to be an advantage in having all of them submitted, as far as likelihood of getting chosen for the award. Joe Nebbia:
