Third Annual American Energy and Manufacturing Competitiveness Summit

Addthis

1 of 5

Assistant Secretary for Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy Dave Danielson addresses the American Energy and Manufacturing Competitiveness Summit held earlier this month at the Arena Stage in Washington, D.C.

Image: Photo courtesy of Attlee Photography

2 of 5

Assistant Secretary Danielson discusses the innovative Mach 1 multi-material lightweight vehicle built through a partnership between Magna International and Ford Motor Company.

Image: Photo courtesy of Attlee Photography

3 of 5

The 50th anniversary 3D-printed Shelby Cobra was featured at the AEMC Summit. Oak Ridge National Laboratory’s Manufacturing Demonstration Facility, EERE’s Advanced Manufacturing Office, Tru-Design, and Cincinnati Incorporated teamed up to make this amazing technology a reality.

Image: Photo courtesy of Attlee Photography

4 of 5

Natel Energy, Inc. exhibited its new hydroEngine™, which enables cost-effective production of low impact, distributed, utility-scale energy from existing non-powered dams, irrigation canals, and other low head hydropower resources.

Image: Photo courtesy of Attlee Photography

5 of 5

Lockheed Martin’s exhibit featured its partnerships with the National Network for Manufacturing Innovation, and included showpieces highlighting advanced composites manufacturing and large scale additive manufacturing. 

Image: Photo courtesy of Attlee Photography
