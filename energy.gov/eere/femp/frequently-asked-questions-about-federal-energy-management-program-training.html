Frequently Asked Questions about Federal Energy Management Program Training

The Federal Energy Management Program (FEMP) provides answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) regarding its training program.

General Topics

Who develops the materials for FEMP training eTraining core courses, First Thursday Seminars, webinars, and in-person workshops?

FEMP courses are developed by the FEMP training team in conjunction with a FEMP technical lead. Our instructors are nationally recognized subject matter experts (SMEs) from the U.S. Department of Energy and its national laboratories and support contractors. These technical leads and SMEs work in conjunction with our experienced course development team to provide training that is both accurate and effectively communicates stated course objectives.

To serve as an instructor for a FEMP training event, individuals are required by FEMP to meet the following minimum qualifications.

  • Given that the content of FEMP courses are technical in nature, instructors will be employed by (or have been recently employed by) FEMP, a national laboratory, other government agency, or a relevant private industry firm with credentials specific to the topic.
  • For all courses involving a review of highly technical content, FEMP instructors should hold a degree, certification, or experience in a relevant field.

For information on your specific course, a brief biography of the instructor will be available on the course description page.

In the FEMP Training Search, what is meant by level, type, format, and topic?

The FEMP Training Search offers search options for the following items.

  • Level: To give our learners a better understanding of the difficulty of accredited courses that cover similar subject matters, FEMP has prescribed introductory, intermediate, or advanced status to each of our courses.
  • Type: FEMP training is delivered using a variety of different types of teaching platforms, including eTraining, First Thursday Seminar, in-person, and webinars. For a more complete definition of each, read about their respective FAQs below.
  • Format: Different FEMP training options are delivered "live" and "on demand." Live courses allow for direct interaction with course instructors, while on-demand courses are prerecorded and available to take at any time.
  • Topic: FEMP training covers a variety of different subject matters pertinent to federal energy managers. FEMP has divided all of its courses into categories to more easily search through its extensive offerings.
What is a FEMP Certificate Series?

A FEMP Certificate Series is a set of similar or related accredited courses that, when completed together, provide the learner with a thorough understanding of the subject matter.

What is included in my Whole Building Design Guide (WBDG) FEMP training learner record?

The WBDG tracks your progress in all the courses that you have started. This allows leaners to keep tabs on the status of completed or uncompleted modules and finish courses at a later date. The pass/fail status of courses in which you have enrolled is also maintained.

The WBDG allows you to view and print your certificate of completion and keep track of IACET CEUs you have earned. To access your learner record, log into WBDG website.

Where can I access my old certificates of completion for FEMP's accredited courses?

The International Association for Continuing Education and Training (IACET) accredited course records can be found by logging into your WBDG account and accessing the course web page. Click "Proceed to Enroll" to access the course on the learning management system. Click the "My Courses" tab, followed by the "Results" tab to display completed course titles. Click "Certificate" beneath the completed course title to access your certificate in as a downloadable PDF. Please note that accredited course offerings include eTraining, First Thursday Seminars, and some on-site courses. Records are not kept for unaccredited courses.

Where can I access my old certificates of completion for non-accredited courses?

Certificates of completion for non-accredited courses are not kept. If you need a certificate of completion for a webinar and it is no longer in your email or records, please contact a FEMP Technical Lead who will advise on how to proceed.

Why do I need to fill out the course evaluation?

The course evaluation is FEMP's only way of receiving course feedback for our on-demand courses. The statistics and comments gleaned from the evaluation help FEMP to continually improve its courses and recognize demand for their continuation.

Why don't I hear any audio?

First, verify that your speakers or headphones are plugged in and turned on. If so, check the volume. If everything appears to be set up properly, consider adjusting your audio settings in your operating system. If you still believe the problem is rooted in the training course and not your personal computer, please reach out to the appropriate course point of contact.

Why does my video or sound freeze?

Video issues and buffering are typically caused by network congestion. Network congestion can occur at any time due to:

  • Slow Internet connection
  • Delays caused by peak Internet traffic hours
  • International connections
  • Heavy traffic on internal company or site networks.

Network traffic and congestion manifests itself in different ways. The sound may work, but the video will freeze; the program continually stops and buffers; or the player automatically stops and requires you to click "play again" to resume.

To alleviate video or sound issues, try minimizing the amount of applications running while accessing the event. If that does not work, consider attending the on-demand version of the seminar, which should be posted shortly after the live event.

 

eTraining Core Courses

What is an eTraining core course?

eTraining core courses are interactive, on-demand, self-paced courses designed in a learning management system. Typically, eTraining courses are split into modules with defined learning objectives to allow learners to stop and then resume their training across long periods of time. FEMP eTraining core courses provide depth and information about the topic, and are eligible for IACET continuing education units (CEUs). eTraining interactive elements include embedded mouse-over informative graphics, mini-quizzes, optional video, and self-pacing lectures.

Are eTraining courses eligible for IACET CEUs?

Yes. To receive a certificate of completion listing the number of CEUs awarded, learners must demonstrate their mastery of the stated learning objectives by achieving 80% on a quiz and completing a course evaluation.

Why do I need a username and password to log in to take an eTraining core course?

A user-specific account allows learners to keep track of the classes they have taken and any CEUs and certificates they have earned. Also, the information provided in the registration process allows FEMP to continually improve its learning events. Finally, creating an account verifies that FEMP training is reaching a real audience, which helps to ensure that FEMP continues to offer free training events in the future.

Where can I take an eTraining core course?

eTraining Core Courses are available on the FEMP Training Search and the WBDG website.

How do I navigate within the eTraining course?

Navigation directions are made clear at the beginning of each eTraining module. Each page contains a navigation bar across the bottom of the page, and participants must click the forward button to advance the slides and view additional material.

Do I have to take the entire course at one time?

No, eTraining courses are paced by the learner and are designed so learners can begin a course, step away, and then resume it at a later time without a problem.

When can I take an eTraining core course?

Now, next week, whenever you want; eTraining core courses are available on-demand anytime.

Can I interact with the instructor?

In completing the course evaluation, learners can indicate that they would like a follow-up from the FEMP technical lead. Coordinating with the course instructor as appropriate, the FEMP technical lead will address any questions, issues, or concerns that you may have.

 

First Thursday Seminars

What is a First Thursday Seminar?

First Thursday Seminars are courses delivered by SMEs using a high-definition television broadcast via Internet streaming. These sessions include a presentation with the SME and are archived for on-demand viewing. Passing the learner assessment following completion of the seminar material allows learners to earn CEUs.

Are First Thursday Seminars eligible for IACET CEUs?

Yes. To receive a certificate of completion listing the number of CEUs awarded, learners must demonstrate their mastery of the stated learning objectives by achieving 80% on a quiz and completing a course evaluation.

How do I register for a live First Thursday Seminar?

Registration for live First Thursday Seminars is available on the seminar's description page on the FEMP Training Search. If no First Thursday Seminars are listed in a search for live courses, continue checking back as these seminars are often in development.

Why haven't I received a confirmation email for a live First Thursday Seminar?

Confirmation emails and webcast links are provided after registering through FEMP Central. Links to the First Thursday Seminar Program are sent the week of the broadcast. If you did not receive a confirmation note following registration, contact femphelp@ee.doe.gov or the listed point of contact. You can register online on the FEMP Training Search.

Where can I find a link to the live First Thursday Seminar webcast?

Webcast links are provided after registering for a First Thursday Seminar. If you did not receive a confirmation note following registration, contact femphelp@ee.doe.gov.

Where can I find links to the First Thursday Seminar exam, evaluation, and certificate?

If you have watched an on-demand First Thursday Seminar on the WBDG, the link to the test is available on the left-hand navigation bar of the launched course viewing window. Click on the link labeled "Test." You must answer 80% of the questions correctly to successfully pass the test and fill out the course evaluation to receive your certificate of completion.

If you have participated in a First Thursday Seminar broadcast on the Knowledge Vision platform, a link to the final quiz, evaluation, and certificate will appear at the end of the program in the lower-right box of your screen. You will also receive an email reminder following the program containing a link to the final quiz, evaluation, and certificate.

Are First Thursday Seminars available on-demand?

First Thursday Seminars are recorded live then posted online for on-demand viewing. FEMP's on-demand First Thursday Seminars can be found on the following web pages:

  • First Thursday Seminars on the FEMP Training Search
  • FEMP Courses on the WBDG.
Why do I need a username and password to log into the test and evaluation for First Thursday Seminars?

We ask that you create an account for a few reasons. The accounts allow you to keep track of the classes you have taken and any CEUs and certificates you have earned. Also, the information you provide in the registration process allows us to continually improve our learning events. Finally, creating an account verifies that FEMP training is reaching a real audience, which helps to ensure that FEMP continues to offer free training events in the future.

 

Live and On-Demand Webinars

Are live and on-demand webinars eligible for IACET CEUs?

No, webinars are typically not eligible for IACET CEUs. However, participants receive a certificate of completion after submitting a course evaluation.

How do I register for a live webinar?

To register for upcoming webinars, visit FEMP Training Search and choose a webinar from the list of live offerings. A link to register for a live webinar is located at the bottom of each webinar description page.

Why can't I see the video or presentation slides on a webinar?

Webinars use a variety of technologies to disseminate information. Please consult your webinar point of contact for help. However, directions for troubleshooting Windows Media Player and the Adobe Flash Player are provided below.

Windows Media Player: Your Windows Media Player may not be installed or configured correctly. Verify that the player has been installed correctly and check the player settings to verify that it is set up to receive the streaming media format. If a broken puzzle piece is displayed, Windows Media Player may not be installed properly. Download Windows Media Player and reinstall it.

If you are within a firewall/proxy server, Windows Media Player may not be using the correct proxy server settings. Symptoms may include messages that read: Could not locate server: xxxxxx or cannot open: xxxxxx. To fix these settings automatically, try reinstalling Windows Media Player. Or you may fix these settings manually as follows:

  • Open Windows Media Player
  • Click the menu and choose "Options"
  • Click on the Network tab. Verify that HTTP has a check next to it
  • Click on the Configure button under the Proxy Settings area
  • When a window with Configure Protocol settings appears, select "Use Browser Proxy Setting"
  • Click "OK."

Adobe Flash Player: The Adobe Flash Player is used in a number of webinar broadcasts. If the video does not begin playing automatically and no loading graphic is displayed, you will need to confirm you have a current version of the player installed. If you do not have the latest version of this free software installed on your computer, you may experience difficulties or be unable to view the stream. You may download the most current version from the Adobe Flash Player website.

If you have downloaded Adobe Flash Player and continue to experience problems viewing the webinar, your internet connection may be too slow to handle the stream.

Where can I find a link to a live webinar?

Webcast links are provided after registration. If you did not receive a confirmation note following registration, contact femphelp@ee.doe.gov or the listed course point of contact.

Why haven't I received a confirmation email for a live webinar?

Confirmation emails and webcast links are provided after registering. If you did not receive a confirmation note following registration, contact femphelp@ee.doe.gov or the listed course point of contact.

Do I need permission from my IT department to view webinars?

Many federal sites require approval to view streaming video over the Internet within site firewalls. Contact your supervisor or IT department to request permission or assistance well before your webinar is scheduled to begin. Your firewall settings may need to be adjusted to accommodate seminar access.

Why do I need a username and password to log in to take an on-demand webinar?

The information provided in the registration process allows FEMP to continually improve its learning events. Also, creating an account verifies that FEMP training is reaching a real audience, which helps to ensure that FEMP continues to offer free training events in the future. Please note that some webinar hosts require additional registration via a third-party hosting platform. Please follow the directions sent before the training event to ensure that you will be able to participate in the webinar.

Where can I find links to the course evaluation and certificate?

FEMP requires learners to complete a course evaluation before awarding them with a certificate. If you have participated in a live webinar, an email reminder will be sent with links to the course evaluation that you must complete before earning a certificate of completion. If you have participated in a recorded webinar, click the "After the Training" section of the webinar description page to take the evaluation and earn your certificate of completion.

 

In-Person Workshops

What is an on-site workshop?

On-site workshops are live, in-person training events. FEMP offers both on-site IACET accredited and non-accredited courses. IACET-accredited courses have a classic traditional classroom lecture model and follow a robust outline. For IACET-accredited courses and targeted advisory sessions, the learner objectives and topics of the session are made clear prior to your participation in the event.

Do in-person workshops offer IACET CEUs?

Sometimes. Accreditation status will be made clear in the announcement of the workshop. The learner objectives and topics of the session will be made clear prior to your participation in the event.

Why haven't I received links to the exam, evaluation, and certificate?

Email reminders with links to the course exam, evaluation, and certificate are sent after each accredited on-site workshop. If you feel there has been an oversight, please reach out to the workshop organizers.

What is the cost of an in-person workshop?

Most on-site courses are free of charge. However, some on-site workshops require a small fee to cover expenses.
