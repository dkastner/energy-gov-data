Featured Publication

Updated Best Practice Guidelines for Residential PACE Financing Programs

On Nov. 18, 2016, DOE released an updated “Best Practice Guidelines for Residential Property Assessed Clean Energy (PACE) Financing Programs,” which provide best practices that can help state and local governments, PACE program administrators, and their partners plan and implement programs that effectively deliver clean energy, water efficiency, and related upgrades to consumers.

The updated best practices reflect input gained from over 200 comments on a draft of the guidelines that was released for public review earlier this summer. It also builds on the PACE financing guidance issued by the Federal Housing Administration and U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs this summer.

A webinar on the updated guidelines will be held on Jan. 5, 2017, at 3–4:30 p.m.

Register for the webinar.

State & Local News

DOE Announces Two New Zero Energy Accelerators

The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) has launched two new zero energy accelerators: the Better Buildings Zero Energy Districts and Zero Energy Schools accelerators to assist local governments in developing more sustainable communities.

In the Zero Energy Districts Accelerator, the zero energy districts are comprised of multi-purpose energy-efficient buildings where the annual energy delivered to buildings is less than or equal to on-site renewable exported energy. DOE is collaborating with the National League of Cities, Rocky Mountain Institute, the U.S. Green Building Council, and EcoDistrict to support Accelerator partners in overcoming deployment barriers by providing a framework for collaboration, a suite of tools, and technical assistance. DOE will work with city leaders, district developers, planners, owners, and additional key stakeholders representing the six partners to develop the business case and energy master planning documents needed to replicate Zero Energy Districts on a broad scale and create a road map for others to follow.

Zero energy districts provide an opportunity to optimize energy efficiency in new construction because of the integration aspects that lead to increased cost savings and environmental and economic benefits. DOE’s multifaceted Zero Energy Building initiative is designed to engage and move the market to buildings and districts that reach zero net energy use.

To see the full scope of this effort or to see if your planned Zero Energy District project qualifies to be a partner, please visit the Zero Energy Districts Accelerator page.

Additionally, on December 6, the Energy Department launched the Better Buildings Zero Energy Schools Accelerator. Six school districts, two states and several national organizations are working collaboratively to develop zero energy design that is cost-competitive to conventional construction in the education sector and in local communities across the nation.

In conjunction with the launch, Energy Department officials joined other key stakeholders to tour Discovery Elementary School, a Zero Energy school located near the nation’s capital in Arlington, Virginia, which officially opened its doors in September 2015. Discovery’s engineering team expects to offset its energy usage with renewable energy and to save about $75,000 within its first year of operation.

The goal is to quickly make Zero Energy K-12 schools more mainstream. Partner school districts commit to developing their own zero energy plans for a district project within a year. They can also engage with fellow states and school districts, and leverage support from regional and national organizations, including the National Renewable Energy Laboratory.

Learn more about the Zero Energy Schools Accelerator.

ESPC and Outdoor Lighting Accelerator Tools Will Guide Future Progress

The Energy Savings Performance Contracts (ESPC) and Outdoor Lighting (OLA) accelerators will sunset on Dec. 31, 2016. Since 2014, ESPC Accelerator partners have realized $2.03 billion in ESPC investment.

The Outdoor Lighting Accelerator secured partnerships with three states, six regional organizations, and 16 cities as partners, and achieved a partner commitment to retrofit 1.3 million light poles.

Toolkits for both accelerators will be released later this month to assist state and local governments with planning and implementing future energy efficiency projects.

The ESPC toolkit will include:

  • The ESPC Virtual Technical Assistant–an online technical assistance resource for ESPC
  • The ESPC Champions Toolkit–a set of resources to mobilize ESPC projects across a large number of state agencies or city/county departments
  • The ESPC Networking Toolkit–a set of resources to engage ESPC stakeholders that are critical for supporting this important vehicle for energy efficiency retrofits
  • An Implementation Model chronicling the experiences of several partners that implemented an innovative approach (Community-Based Social Marketing) to energizing ESPC in typically underserved market.

Highlights of the OLA toolkit include:

  • Outdoor Lighting Decision Tree Tool–Consistently rated as a top-10 viewed resource on the State and Local Solution Center
  • Outdoor Lighting Challenges and Solutions Pathways Report–Examples of LED street light conversion projects
  • Issue Briefs
  • Comparison tool for municipally owned vs. utility-owned steetlights
  • Connected Lighting Webinar
  • and more.
New Leading by Example Partnership Network Launched

States are invited to join a new listserv, the Leading by Example Interstate (LBE) Network, that will enable states to communicate directly and efficiently about leading by example programs, challenges, successes and opportunities. The network was originally conceived at an ad hoc meeting of several states during the 2015 Better Buildings Summit in Washington, D.C., and represents a collaboration among DOE, the National Association of State Energy Offices (NASEO), and the National Governors Association (NGA) in an effort to support improved communication and collaboration among states.

The LBE Network listserv is meant to help states communicate more effectively about LBE programs, such as efforts leading to clean energy and sustainability efforts within state government facilities. While we recognize that states have clean energy efforts that target consumers and businesses, as well as broader energy policy concerns, we believe LBE program leads would benefit from a coordinated way to communicate with one another and share their challenges and successes. We hope that this new listserv will encourage regular questioning and information sharing among policymakers and program managers that will be helpful in creating and improving LBE programs. It is being launched for state and federal staff working on LBE issues.

Follow these steps to join:

  1. Request an invitation from Todd Sims at NASEO
  2. Follow the link in your email invitation 
  3. Complete the registration form with your contact information and photo (optional).

LBNL Releases New Report on Current Practices in Energy Financing

Funded by WIP, Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL) released a new report, "Current Practices in Efficiency Financing," which helps guide customer-facing financing products—products offered by a lender directly to a borrower—used to pay for energy efficiency.

State and local governments need to understand the full range of financing product options for target participants, the tradeoffs of various products, and potential advantages and disadvantages for different types of customers when deciding whether to start a new program, tune up an existing program, or create a Green Bank.

On November 17, WIP held a webinar to discuss the report and a summary of market barriers and products that address those barriers by customer sector. Speakers included authors Greg Leventis of LBNL and Chris Kramer of Energy Futures Group. View the webinar video and presentation.

State Energy Program Funding Helps Arizona Conserve Energy at Water Resource Recovery Facilities

In 2015, three Arizona water treatment facilities underwent partial upgrades thanks to the Energy Department’s State Energy Program funding. Arizona was competitively selected to further its goal of reducing energy consumption at the state’s wastewater facilities by 22% by 2020 from a 2010 baseline. The state has achieved 2% energy savings and 2.4% cost savings. The retrofits are projected to achieve 9% energy savings, and reduce approximately 5 million pounds of carbon dioxide emissions annually. Read more.

K–12 Schools Charging Toward Clean Energy Goals

With over 25 school districts signed up for the Better Buildings Challenge, covering 15,000 buildings, and over 225 million square feet, these partners have taken on and overcome various barriers to advancing clean energy goals. K-12 School Districts across the country continue to face student population growth, aging infrastructure, rising energy costs, reduced annual operating budgets, and increased plug load demands in the classroom. Energy efficiency provides potential to return savings back to educating students while establishing safe, healthy, and productive environments for learning. Read more about these efforts.

Better Buildings SWAP Features Navy Versus Air Force

DOE launched season two of "Better Buildings Challenge SWAP," featuring the U.S. Naval Academy and U.S. Air Force Academy. Both military operations swap energy teams to improve the energy efficiency of each one's campus.

"The Better Buildings Challenge SWAP has proven to be a powerful new way to reach those who can create future energy improvements in our nation’s buildings,” said Kathleen Hogan, Deputy Assistant Secretary for Energy Efficiency. “The reality style of the show combined with opportunities to go behind the scenes with some of our nation’s most respected organizations and energy leaders brings a fresh new light to energy efficiency efforts in U.S. buildings."

Through the Better Buildings Challenge SWAP, DOE is helping commercial and industrial organizations successfully explore and share new ways to reduce energy use by their organizations. Stay tuned for Season 3 of Better Buildings Challenge SWAP coming in early 2017 that will feature the cities of Atlanta and Boston. Learn more about the Better Buildings Challenge SWAP series.
