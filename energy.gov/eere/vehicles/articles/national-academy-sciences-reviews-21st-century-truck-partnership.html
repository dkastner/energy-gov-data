National Academy of Sciences Reviews 21st Century Truck Partnership

January 29, 2016 - 11:54am

Addthis

Freightliner's SuperTruck, which improved Class 8 truck efficiency by 115%. The National Academy of Sciences reported the four SuperTruck projects are “impressive” and will “significantly reduce the fuel consumption of Class 8 tractor-trailer vehicles."

For more than 15 years, the 21st Century Truck Partnership (21CTP) has been facilitating progress in the energy efficiency, emissions, and safety of U.S. trucks and buses.  By accelerating collaborative research and development among government and industry partners, including the Vehicle Technologies Office, 21CTP aims to enable America’s medium and heavy-duty vehicles to safely and cost-effectively move ever larger volumes of freight and number of passengers while minimizing pollution and the dependency on foreign oil. VTO regularly works with the National Academy of Sciences (NAS) to evaluate 21CTP's program direction, assess technical progress towards meeting the stated goals, and offer suggestions to improve the Partnership.

NAS recently released its “Review of the 21st Century Truck Partnership: Third Report,” in which it offered a very favorable review of 21CTP and its accomplishments, particularly those related to the very successful SuperTruck initiative, which focuses on developing Class 8 tractor-trailers that are 50% more efficient than baseline models by 2015. 

Highlights from the NAS report include:

  • The four SuperTruck projects are “impressive” and will “significantly reduce the fuel consumption of Class 8 tractor-trailer vehicles, which consume the greatest part of the fuel used in the United States for heavy vehicles.”
  • The SuperTruck work brings together “a wide variety of companies, the national laboratories, and universities.”
  • The Partnership has achieved its goal of a 50 percent brake thermal efficiency (BTE) engine, and is developing a pathway to 55 percent BTE.
  • The Partnership has made “good progress” in its efforts to improve communication among its partners, and documenting its projects and budgets.

NAS also offered some suggestions on areas for improvement or future consideration to help make the Partnership better:

  • NAS suggested that 21CTP review its research goals across its technology areas, and make revisions if needed based on the funding available within the federal agencies to reach these goals.
  • NAS encouraged 21CTP to use its proposed expansion of the hybrid team’s scope to “lead to vehicle efficiency and emissions reduction improvements via a range of technology options.”
  • NAS recommended that 21CTP consider developing specific goals for emissions aftertreatment systems.

NAS publishes its technical analysis and review findings in an extensive final report on the review. The most recent report is available on the National Academies’ website.  The report brief offers a four-page summary of the report’s conclusions.
