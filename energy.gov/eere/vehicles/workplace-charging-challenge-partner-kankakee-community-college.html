Workplace Charging Challenge Partner: Kankakee Community College

Joined the Challenge: June 2014
Headquarters: Kankakee, IL
Charging Locations: Bradley, IL
Domestic Employees: 546

In 2007, Kankakee Community College (KCC) set a goal to make sustainability a locally and regionally recognized feature of the college. This same year, they also signed the American College and University Presidents’ Climate Commitment and the Illinois Sustainable Campus Compact, and hired a Dean for Environmental and Institutional Sustainability. Workplace charging helps KCC achieve its mission by increasing sustainability literacy while reaching carbon neutrality by 2041. KCC currently has one Level 2 electric vehicle supply equipment (EVSE) installed at its LEED Gold North Extension Center and plans to install two additional EVSE at its Riverfront Campus.

Electric Vehicle Road Trip

A relay of electric vehicles will cross more than 450 miles of Illinois, stopping in Bradley at 8 a.m. Wednesday, June 25.

The "EV Road Trip" will begin in Carterville June 23 and will make 12 promotional stops along its way to Grayslake on June 25. The Bradley stop will be at the Kankakee Community College North Extension Center, 450 N. Kinzie Ave. Refreshments will be served from 8-8:45 a.m.

Eleven community colleges, plus the Governor's Mansion in Springfield, are on the route.

"It's nice to be part of a statewide demonstration that electric vehicles are here to stay and that it's possible to drive all around the state," said Bert Jacobsen, KCC dean of environmental and institutional sustainability. "There are enough charging stations along the route from south to north Illinois that you could get partial charges all the way from one end of the state to the other."

The trip begins at John A. Logan College, the southernmost "charged" community college in Illinois. From there, electric cars will go from college to college, with a new driver and car taking over at many of the colleges along the way. Eight electric cars will be used. The stops, in order, are John A. Logan College, Carterville; Kaskaskia College, Centralia; Southwestern Illinois College, Belleville; Lewis & Clark Community College, Godfrey; Governor’s Mansion, Springfield; Heartland Community College, Normal; KCC, Bradley; Prairie State College, Chicago Heights; Moraine Valley Community College, Palos Hills; Triton College, River Grove; and Wilbur Wright College of Chicago. The relay includes some short drives to visit charging stations in Centralia, Lincoln and Pontiac.

The longest leg will be 93 miles from Lewis and Clark Community College to the Governor's Mansion. The shortest segment between colleges is 5.4 miles from Triton College to Wilbur Wright College.

Hove Nissan of Bradley is loaning KCC a Nissan Leaf to use for the local leg of the journey. The Leaf will depart from the electric car power station in the parking lot at the KCC North Extension Center before setting off for Prairie State College.

"Our charging station is a Chargepoint® 240 volt level 2 charging station," Jacobson said. “Which is not the highest level so it would take about four hours for a full charge. But we're excited about this relay because first of all it's going to be fun. It gives us an opportunity among our colleagues from across the state to do something together. It also gives us an opportunity to highlight our new North Extension Center that’s a LEED gold building with an EV charging station."

For more information, contact Jacobson at bjacobson@kcc.edu 815-802-8242.

The electric vehicle road trip is being sponsored by the Illinois Green Economy Network (IGEN), a consortium to grow the green economy of Illinois which includes all 39 community college districts and 48 campuses of Illinois community colleges.

For further information on sustainability efforts at KCC and other Illinois Community Colleges, contact Jacobson at 815-802-8242 or visit www.kcc.edu/sustainability/.

Sidebar:
Illinois offers incentives for electric vehicle ownership

  • Illinois Environmental Protection Act Alternative Fuel Rebate Program (10% of the vehicle suggested retail price, up to $4,000).
  • Federal Tax Credit of $7,500.00 for electric vehicles.
  • Discounted vehicle registration fees for fully electric vehicles.
  • EV Charging Station network.
