Workplace Charging Equipment Costs

Cost estimates for different tiers of Level 2 EVSE units. Image from Kristina Rivenbark, NWT.

Charging stations are available from a variety of manufacturers in a range of models for all charging applications. For a single port charging station, Level 1 hardware costs range from $300-$1,500 and Level 2 hardware costs range from $400-$6,500. DC Fast chargers, which are used extremely rarely for workplace charging, range from $10,000-$40,000. Charging station costs are affected by a number of cost drivers, including: the charging level, the number of charging ports, the type of mounting system and additional features beyond the basic charging station.

Charging Level: The charging power of a unit depends on its amperage rating - the higher the amperage, the more power the unit can deliver. As it is more expensive to manufacture higher amperage chargers, Level 1 is the least expensive, Level 2 units are midrange, and DC fast charging is the most expensive.

Charging Ports: The number of charging ports on a charging station dictates the number of vehicles that can be plugged in concurrently. Stations are available in single- and multi-port configurations. Only one vehicle at a time can plug into a single-port unit. Multi-port units, which commonly have 2, 3, or 4 ports, can: charge vehicles simultaneously or sequentially, or offer multiple charging voltages. (They can also have multiple connector standards, but this is only relevant for DC fast charging.) Considering total upfront costs, multi-port units are more expensive than single-port units, but on a per-port basis, multi-ports are generally cheaper.

Mounting System: Charging stations can either be wall-mounted or mounted on a pedestal located near the parking spot. Wall-mounted stations are generally used in situations where the parking space is located near an adjacent wall, such as inside a parking garage. Pedestal mounts provide more design flexibility, as they can be mounted in parking lots or other locations. The installation process for a pedestal mounted station is more costly than a wall-mounted station.

Additional Features: A basic charging stations safely supply electricity to vehicles and informs the driver if the unit is charging the vehicle. "Smart" charging stations provide many additional features, described below, that can benefit both drivers and the charging station owners. However, typically, the more features a charging station has, the more expensive it is to purchase and maintain.

  • Communication Capabilities: Charging stations can be connected to many different networks. They can communicate with to the user, site hose, utility grid, and the Internet. Networked stations are connected to the Internet and transmit operational data back to the company that manages the network. Many of the other features below depend on the communication capabilities of the station.
  • Access Control: Access control mechanisms can be installed on charging stations to restrict the usage of the unit to a predesignated group. Systems range from simple, e.g., lock or keypad, to more complex, e.g., access by radio-frequency identification (RFID) cards or through smartphone applications.
  • Point of Sale (POS): The charging station can calculate the amount owed by a customer and provide payment methods. These could include payments by credit card, RFID, or smartphone application.
  • Energy Monitoring and Management: Charging stations can track energy consumption and provide reports on greenhouse gas emission reduction. Equipped stations can also optimize the energy load to maximize charging during low rate periods and minimize charging during high rate periods.
