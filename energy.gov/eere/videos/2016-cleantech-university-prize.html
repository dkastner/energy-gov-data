2016 Cleantech University Prize

Addthis

Description 
Below is the text version for the "2016 Cleantech University Prize" video.

Text Version

Text on screen:

21 Teams

Present their big idea for the next clean energy breakthrough

3 Winning Teams

Share $100,000 to being their ideas to life

David Friedman, Acting Assistant Secretary for the Office of Energy Efficiency & Renewable Energy:

When I think about our energy future…I feel nothing but optimism because I know that there's a clean energy revolution underway right now, where great minds have been driving down the cost of technologies from solar power and wind power to batteries and LEDs and one of the great things about these competitions is we are fostering that next generation of innovators, who are going to accelerate that clean energy revolution so that we can live in a world where climate change is a thing of the past, where energy dependence is a thing of the past, where we can create more jobs, where we can grow our economy, and we're seeing success…we've had several teams in the past who've gone on to get significant investments to turn their great ideas in to great products that can solve real problems.

Jennifer Garson, Manager, Cleantech University Prize, Office of Energy Efficiency & Renewable Energy:

I think what competitions like this provide is an opportunity for technologists, in particular, to step outside of their labs and be able to think about growing their businesses…everybody here, all the judges they want to see all these entrepreneurs succeed. In fact, one of the hardest parts about our job is picking one or two or three winners…but what it gives students an opportunity to do is to see what it would feel like to actually go out and pitch your company but in a more comfortable setting.

John Dommel, 2016 Participant, Cleantech University Prize:

The most challenging part for me…is getting out of my comfort zone…you know coming out of the lab and really talking to people, meeting people…trying to explain what I've worked on for five years...in two minutes and that's been tricky but rewarding at the same time.

Jennifer Garson:

Getting it to hear these pitches makes me absolutely excited about what's to come for the future of clean tech and not just from the pitches that I've heard here...but I still work with some of our entrepreneurs who participated years ago, who are out there fundamentally changing the world.

John Dommel:

I mean you just read the news and it's all bleary and it's all, "oil's gonna kill us all and the CO2 in the environment" and it's so exciting to see so many people so smart and so dedicated to solving these problems...and it's done incrementally, right? Each one of these is a small device and it's a small step but it's solving a big problem and together, I hope that we'll have an impact.

Text on screen: Learn more at cleantechup.org.
