December 11-12, 2014 - Technical Exchange Meeting - Las Vegas, Nevada

On December 11-12, 2014, the Depart of Energy (DOE) Office of Environmental Management, along with the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC), Washington State Department of Ecology, and Commonwealth of Kentucky, conducted the Annual Technical Exchange for the Interagency Steering Committee on Performance and Risk Assessment Community of Practice (P&RA CoP).  The theme of the 2014 Technical Exchange was “Best Practices in Performance and Risk Assessments in Support of Risk-Informed, Cost-Effective Environmental Management Decisions.”  The meeting was held in the National Atomic Testing Museum in Las Vegas, NV, and attracted more than 70 CoP members and other interested parties from DOE EM headquarters and field offices, NRC, EPA, regulators from Washington, Kentucky, Nevada and Texas states, national labs, universities, and the private sector.  The DOE Associate Deputy Assistant Secretary for Site Restoration Bill Levitan opened the meeting with remarks on management expectations for the group, followed by keynote presentations by Dr. Jennifer Heimberg on recent National Academy of Sciences Workshops on Best Practices for Risk-Informed Decision Making Regarding Contaminated Sites, and by Dr. David Kosson on the Consortium for Risk Evaluation with Stakeholder Partnership (CRESP)-led Hanford Site-wide Risk Review.  Then the group participated in discussions of a variety of topical studies and site applications that were led by 14 speakers, and provided feedbacks on planning of Calendar Year 2015 activities. 

With support from the National Nuclear Security Administration’s Nevada Field Office, a tour of the Nevada National Security Site was also provided to 25 meeting participants on December 10, 2014.
