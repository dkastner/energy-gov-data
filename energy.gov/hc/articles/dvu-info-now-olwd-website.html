DVU info now on the OLWD website

July 31, 2015 - 3:00pm

Addthis

The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) Virtual University (DVU) was a website that provided employees cost-effective and user-friendly options for professional development and training 24/7. The DVU started in 2012 with four colleges; over nearly four years, it grew to seven partnering colleges from across the DOE mission areas and had 4,600 visitors per month.

In line with the new HR service delivery model, the Office of Learning & Workforce Development (OLWD) is redesigning its mission and structure, and DVU will transform to reflect the new service delivery model. Since April 2015, the DVU & OLWD (“HC-20”) websites have been merging to create a new site for learning and development activities for the DOE workforce.  This new site will increase customer satisfaction with increased usability and alignment with the new OLWD delivery model.

Our new site will continue to support DOE and Human Capital goals by:

  • Providing a website that is user-friendly and easily accessible to all geographically separated DOE organizations.
  • Leveraging corporate resources to more effectively and efficiently meet training needs that support organizational missions across the department.
  • Providing a centralized location where learning and development opportunities, services, and support are made available to all DOE employees.
  • Redesigning L&D information, to align with the OLWD mission to strengthen the DOE workforce capacity and build a culture of continual learning and knowledge sharing.

By consolidating DVU and HC-20 websites, learning and workforce development services and support can be shared and partnerships established to reduce redundancy and ultimately save resources.  This common DOE-wide approach will result in a fully integrated, learning community across the Department.
