Thirteen Alaska Community Efficiency Champions Selected to Receive Technical Assistance from the Energy Department

April 26, 2016 - 12:58pm

Addthis

Christopher Clark Deschene
Director, Office of Indian Energy Policy and Programs

Today, at the 2016 Alaska Rural Energy Conference in Fairbanks, I had the pleasure of announcing 13 communities selected to receive technical assistance as part of the Remote Alaska Communities Energy Efficiency (RACEE) Competition. The RACEE Competition is a $4 million joint effort between the U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Indian Energy and the Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy focused on significantly accelerating efforts by remote Alaskan communities to adopt sustainable energy strategies.

The communities selected to receive technical assistance are part of a larger group of 64 RACEE Community Efficiency Champions pledging to reduce per-capita energy use 15% by 2020. Through these pledges, DOE heard directly from remote Alaskan communities and Native villages about the impacts of high energy costs, which have been driven up to unsustainable levels by the limited accessibility of their remote locations. Many of these communities also face high poverty and unemployment rates, so the high costs of energy create an even more disproportionate economic burden.

The Community Efficiency Champions are small, remote communities ranging in population from 34 to 3,200, with 10 villages of less than 100 people, and only five communities with more than 1,000 residents. As part of the competition, the Champions gain access to a peer network intended to empower communities with information and tools to make more informed, strategic decisions regarding their energy future.

The 13 communities selected in the second phase will work with technical assistance providers to develop project plans to meet or exceed the pledged 15% energy reduction. This technical assistance is intended to position the communities to successfully obtain loans or compete for funding to implement their plans.

The Champions selected to receive technical assistance are:

  • City of Akutan
  • Village of Chefornak
  • City of False Pass
  • City of Galena
  • Village of Holy Cross
  • Village of Kiana
  • Village of Klawock
  • City of Kotlik
  • City of Noorvik
  • City of Port Lions
  • City of Ruby
  • City of Sand Point
  • City of Shishmaref

These communities will receive personalized technical assistance based on their applications to support activities such as performing residential energy audits, waste water treatment facility planning, conducting commercial building audits, exploring ways to decrease line losses, and enact codes and policy to support integrated renewable power generation.

This summer, these Champions will enter the final phase of the RACEE Competition and be eligible to compete for a total of up to $3.4 million in funding from DOE to implement their energy efficiency plans. In October 2016, DOE expects to award funding to help up to five communities implement their plans to reach their energy pledge reductions.
