Strategic Energy Planning

The U.S. Department of Energy Office of Indian Energy provides tribes and Alaska Native communities with technical assistance and resources for strategic energy planning.

A strategic energy plan clearly identifies a community’s energy priorities and can provide valuable guidance for decision making and better position a community for new opportunities to increase tribal self-sufficiency and meet tribal energy goals. 

Apply for Technical Assistance

Tribes and Alaska Native villages can apply for strategic energy planning technical assistance that includes an on-site workshop designed to assist tribal leaders and staff in understanding their resources, identifying their options, and developing a viable road map to successful project completion. The workshop is facilitated by tribal energy experts and walks attendees through the nine-step strategic energy planning process outlined below. 

Strategic Energy Planning Process

The nine-step process for community strategic energy planning helps tribes establish a comprehensive understanding of current energy use and costs, get insight into efficiency and conservation strategies already underway, and identify valuable opportunities to further reduce future energy costs through new programs and activities. The strategic energy planning process is a framework that can be modified to meet each tribal community’s need, which is critical to a tribe’s future as it provides the necessary foundation for long-term support of energy project development.

STEP 1: IDENTIFY AND CONVENE STAKEHOLDERS

Anyone who generates, controls the sale of, sells, or uses electricity or gas is a critical stakeholder. By involving all stakeholders early in the planning stages, tribes increase their chances of successful project development.

STEP 2: FORM A LEADERSHIP TEAM

It is important to choose a leadership team that has the power to make decisions, direct funding resources, and promote the project throughout the process.

STEP 3: DEVELOP A COMMON ENERGY VISION

Identifying top priorities can help develop a vision that will ensure a unified effort.

STEP 4: ASSESS ENERGY NEEDS AND RESOURCES

Understanding energy use at the local level helps clarify which programs and projects will best fit the needs of the community. Develop a community energy baseline to help design cost/benefit rankings for potential programs. The DOE State and Local Energy Data tool provides easy access to energy data by location.

STEP 5: DEVELOP SPECIFIC ENERGY GOALS

Energy goals should be based on the community’s vision and energy baseline.

STEP 6: PRIORITIZE PROJECTS AND PROGRAMS

Identify and evaluate supply-and-demand policy and program resource options and match these to the goals and program rankings.

STEP 7: IDENTIFY FINANCING OPTIONS

Various options are available to finance energy projects, including federal, state, and non-profit grants and loans.

STEP 8: COMPILE THE ENERGY PLAN

An energy plan includes objectives, goals, a baseline, program options and surrounding analysis, and recommended options for policymakers.

STEP 9: MEASURE AND EVALUATE, MODIFY PLAN

Is there a current plan for program evaluation? How high is the demand for a formal document or announcement? How will the results be communicated back to the individual program implementers?

Handbooks

The Office of Indian Energy has developed two strategic energy planning handbooks—one for tribes in the 48 contiguous states and one for Alaska Native villages—that can be used as a roadmap for discussion and decisions related to strategic energy planning and energy project prioritization. The handbooks include blank text boxes for communities to input their own information and outcomes from energy planning discussions.
