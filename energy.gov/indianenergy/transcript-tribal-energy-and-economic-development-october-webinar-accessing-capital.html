Joel Laubenstein:       Sure – and a good question because patience and persistence are definitely required as part of the new markets process. So from, you know, no different than going out and trying to get a loan or a commitment from an equity investor, you take your project, and you put a – essentially, do initial preliminary evaluation to confirm it's a good candidate or everybody thinks this is a good candidate to go get credits.

 

                                    From there, you put a little package together that you "shop" to these CDEs. Once those term sheets are executed, it's really a march toward a closing just like any other loan or financial transaction closing to get all the due diligence completed, all the documents in place, and ultimately an injection of capital to the borrower or through the project for use in the project.
