Laboratory Operations Board

The Department of Energy operates a nationwide system of 17 National Laboratories that comprise the most comprehensive research network of its kind in the world.  The Laboratory Operations Board (LOB) provides an enterprise-wide forum for addressing operational and management improvements in areas that impact the National Laboratories.

Mission

The objectives of the Laboratory Operations Board (LOB) are to strengthen and enhance the partnership between the Department of Energy and its National Laboratories, and to improve management and performance in order to more effectively and efficiently execute the missions of the DOE and the National Laboratories.

Organization

The LOB is chaired by the Under Secretary for Management and Performance, is managed by a LOB Director, and its membership includes senior Federal and Laboratory employees. 

LOB Initiatives

 The LOB is responsible for certain DOE-wide initiatives, including the following:

CRENEL Implementation. The LOB is the primary Departmental entity responsible for tracking the Department’s efforts to implement the commitments to strengthen the DOE-laboratory partnership, as outlined in the Department’s February 2016 Report to Congress in response to the Commission to Review the Effectiveness of the National Energy Laboratories (CRENEL).  In that response, the Department committed that the LOB would conduct a review of the effectiveness of CRENEL Implementation before February 2018.  In addition, in the fall of 2016 the former CRENEL co-chairs reviewed the Department’s progress in implementing the commitments it made in its February 2016 response to the CRENEL report.  The letters from TJ Glauthier and Jared Cohon reflecting their individual reviews are available here.

Annual State of General Purpose Infrastructure Report. This report is developed by the Infrastructure Executive Committee, a subgroup of the LOB.  The inaugural report was issued in 2016, and it will be distributed on an annual basis by the end of each Fiscal Year.  This report helps inform investment priorities as well as the effectiveness and efficiency of DOE real property management.

Biannual Excess Facilities Report (Plan for Deactivation and Decommissioning of Nonoperational Defense Nuclear Facilities).  This biannual report to Congress is developed by the Excess Contaminated Facilities Working Group, a subgroup of the LOB.  This report, which contains enterprise-wide data on DOE excess facilities, was issued in December 2016 and will be updated every two years.

Annual State of the National Laboratories Report.  This annual report to Congress describes the National Laboratory system, discusses its role and value, and identifies the actions being pursued to enhance the vitality of the laboratory system to help ensure that it continues to provide best-in-class science and technology research and solutions to meet the near-term and long-term missions of the Department.  The LOB facilitates the collaborative effort of the Under Secretary offices to develop and issue this report.  The inaugural report was released in January 2017 and annual updates will be issued each fall.

LOB Spotlight - Addressing aging DOE infrastructure and excess facilities

The Department is facing a systemic challenge of degrading infrastructure due to the age of the DOE complex – which dates back to the Manhattan project – and levels of deferred maintenance that have been increasing.  DOE deferred maintenance for its active assets was at $5.4 billion at the end of FY2015.  The LOB transformed the way the Department assesses and manages infrastructure, leading a first-ever enterprise-wide assessment of general purpose infrastructure across all 17 labs and NNSA sites and plants, using newly-established uniform metrics.  The LOB effort revealed that approximately half of the assessed infrastructure is rated as not “adequate” to meet the current mission needs.  Using this data, the Department established an enterprise-wide set of priorities for general purpose infrastructure, focusing on the goals of reducing deferred maintenance, increasing reliability, reducing facility footprint, and reducing risk to safety and to mission.  This LOB-led initiative provided the basis for additional funding targeted for prioritized general purpose infrastructure projects.

With respect to the challenges posed by excess contaminated facilities, the Excess Contaminated Facilities Working Group, which is a subgroup of the LOB, developed and executed an enterprise-wide data collection effort to obtain updated cost and risk assessments to deactivate, decontaminate, decommission, and demolish excess facilities.  The updated data from the working group was used to define the scope of the challenge and to identify options for how DOE may better prioritize excess facilities. The group developed a report, which was issued in 2016, and will be updated every two years.
