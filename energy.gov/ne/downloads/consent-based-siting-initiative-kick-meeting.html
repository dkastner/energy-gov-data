Consent-Based Siting Initiative Kick-off Meeting

Consent-Based Siting Kick-off Meeting

Meeting Summary

On January 20, 2016, the Department of Energy held a kick-off meeting for its consent-based siting initiative. More than 120 people, representing a range of stakeholders, attended the meeting in Washington, DC with another 200 individuals participating remotely by webinar and conference call. The event featured a keynote address by Dr. Lynn Orr, the Department’s Undersecretary for Science and Energy, followed by a panel discussion and question and answer session that included John Kotek, Acting Assistant Secretary for Nuclear Energy; Dr. William Boyle, Director of the Office of Used Nuclear Fuel Disposition; and Andrew Griffith, Associate Deputy Assistant Secretary for Fuel Cycle Technologies.

In addition, meeting materials, including an agenda and a written record of the event can be found at the bottom of this page.

Keynote Address

The meeting began with a keynote address by Dr. Lynn Orr, the Department’s Under Secretary for Science and Energy. In his remarks, Dr. Orr emphasized the importance of meeting long-term nuclear waste management needs to ensure that the nuclear energy industry remains viable and nuclear power continues to provide the nation with safe, sustainable, and responsible electricity. Dr. Orr also reviewed the history of nuclear waste facility siting challenges in the United States and emphasized present opportunities. Dr. Orr referenced the 2012 report by the Blue Ribbon Commission on America’s Nuclear Future, which recommended a consent-based approach after conducting a comprehensive review of waste management policies. Key findings from this report helped inform the Administration’s 2013 Strategy for the Management and Disposal of Used Nuclear Fuel and High-Level Radioactive Waste. Dr. Orr described the components of an integrated waste management system laid out in the Strategy, including  pilot interim storage facilities, larger consolidated interim storage facilities, and geologic repositories to dispose of spent nuclear fuel and high-level radioactive waste. To support development of this integrated waste management system, Dr. Orr emphasized that the Department’s consent-based siting initiative would focus on a strong and ongoing public engagement effort. Dr. Orr announced that the first two public meetings are scheduled for March 2016 in Chicago and April 2016 in Atlanta.

Panel Discussion

Following Dr. Orr’s remarks, three team members from the Department’s Office of Nuclear Energy discussed their efforts in developing a consent-based siting process and planning for an integrated waste management system. The first speaker was Mr. John Kotek, Acting Assistant Secretary for Nuclear Energy. Mr. Kotek recapped his experience working with the Blue Ribbon Commission, as well as siting wind farms and other similar installations. In his remarks, Mr. Kotek emphasized the value of public engagement and its importance in successful facility siting efforts. With respect to the Department’s plans for consent based-siting, Mr. Kotek described the effort as consisting of three phases: (i) initial public engagement to gauge stakeholder opinions and thoughts regarding a consent-based siting process; (ii) the design of a consent-based siting process based on this input; and (iii) work with potential host communities using the consent-based process. Finally, Mr. Kotek introduced key members of the Department’s staff who are supporting the consent-based siting effort and encouraged audience members and Department staff to get to know each other and begin a constructive dialogue. 

Dr. William Boyle, Director of the Office of Used Nuclear Fuel Disposition discussed the technical aspects of the Department’s ongoing activities, ranging from site selection criteria to transportation, routing studies, and emergency preparedness.  Dr. Boyle highlighted specific areas of study including extended storage studies, transportation modelling of high burn-up fuel, and characterization of geologic media supporting disposal.  Dr. Boyle also referenced the deep borehole R&D effort, including the field test that was recently announced and will take place in Rugby, North Dakota.

The final panel speaker was Mr. Andrew Griffith, Associate Deputy Assistant Secretary for Fuel Cycle Technologies. Mr. Griffith’s talk focused on the public engagement efforts in support of the consent-based siting initiative. Mr. Griffith described the Department’s public engagement efforts in three elements. The first is an extended public comment opportunity, which began in December with the Invitation for Public Comment published in the Federal Register. Mr. Griffith encouraged all stakeholders and interested parties to submit their comments. The second element is a broader public engagement effort to include public meetings such as the ones in Chicago (March) and Atlanta (April), webinars, and conference calls. In addition, Mr. Griffith mentioned the Department’s participation in two near-term events: the February 2016 meeting of the Energy Communities Alliance and the March 2016 Waste Management Symposium meeting in Phoenix, Arizona. The final element Mr. Griffith discussed was the opportunity to meet with interested groups in smaller forums. For those organizations wishing to engage with the Department directly, Mr. Griffith invited them to reach out to the Department directly via email at consentbasedsiting@hq.doe.gov.

Questions and Answers

Following the panel presentation, the meeting moved to a question and answer session. Questions were fielded from the audience as well as from those on the webinar. The questions were divided into four categories: consent-based siting, transportation, storage and disposal, and other. During the 60-minute question and answer period, the panel members answered approximately 30 questions.

Poster Session

At the conclusion of the question and answer session, Mr. Kotek thanked the audience for their participation and the formal meeting was adjourned. Audience members were then invited to attend a poster session where nine informational posters were on display, each of which was supported by a team member from the Department of Energy. The poster session lasted approximately one hour and was attended by approximately 100 individuals.
