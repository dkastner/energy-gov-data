About Us

Shahram Ghasemian - Director, The Alternative Dispute Resolution Office

Shahram Ghasemian is the Director of the Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) Office which resides in the Office of Hearings and Appeals.  The ADR Office provides mediation, training and other ADR services, and promotes the use of alternative dispute resolution techniques, at all levels of the DOE complex. 

Mr. Ghasemian joined DOE in 2015 after 9 years at the Nuclear Regulatory Commission where he oversaw the day-to-day activities of the Office of Enforcement’s Alternative Dispute Resolution Program in addition to handling enforcement actions, and assisting senior management on policy issues relating to the NRC’s Employee Protection regulations.  During his tenure at the NRC, he acted in other capacities including as a senior staff attorney and Branch Chief in several organizations.  While on detail from the NRC, he also served on the U.S. Senator Tom Carper's staff from 2013-2014 as a nuclear energy policy fellow.

Prior to joining the NRC in 2006, Mr. Ghasemian served as Assistant General Counsel for USEC Inc., an NRC certificate-holder for approximately 9 years.  In that capacity, he advised senior management on NRC regulated activities, employment matters, commercial transactions and corporate compliance and governance issues.  He also litigated “whistleblower” complaints before the Department of Labor and the NRC.  From 1999 to 2001, he served as Chief Counsel at USEC’s production facility in Paducah, Kentucky.

Mr. Ghasemian received his B.S. in Civil and Environmental Engineering from the University of Wisconsin in 1990 and his J.D. from the John Marshall Law School in Chicago, Illinois, in 1997, where he was an editor on the John Marshall Law Review.  Mr. Ghasemian is admitted to practice law in the Commonwealth of Virginia and the District of Columbia.
