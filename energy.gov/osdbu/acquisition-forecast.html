Acquisition Forecast

It is the policy of the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) to provide timely information to the public regarding DOE’s forecast of future prime contracting opportunities and subcontracting opportunities which are available via the Department’s major site and facilities management contractors. This forecast has been expanded to also provide timely status information for ongoing prime contracting actions that are valued in excess of the simplified acquisition threshold. As the largest civilian contracting agency within the Federal government, the Department believes that enhancing the forecast to provide a greater level of advance and up-to-date information pertaining to the Department’s future and ongoing contracting opportunities will substantially improve the nature of and accessibility to the kind of procurement information that is important to DOE management, the public, industry, Congress, and other DOE stakeholders.

Information contained in the forecast for future and ongoing transactions can be searched and sorted in a number of ways, including by DOE contracting activity, state, type of acquisition, and North American Industrial Classification Code. In addition, points of contact are identified for each opportunity that is listed for access to additional information. 

The acquisitions that are identified in this forecast are expected to be solicited and/or awarded in this fiscal year and beyond, based on the best information available at the time of publication. All projected acquisitions and associated information are subject to revision or cancellation. Final decisions regarding the extent of competition, socio-economic considerations, estimated value, etc., will not be made until each acquisition is formally initiated. The data and information that is contained in this forecast should be considered for informational and planning purposes only – it does not represent a pre-solicitation synopsis, nor does it constitute an invitation for bid or request for proposal, or a commitment by the Government to purchase the described supplies and services. 

For specific information on doing business with the DOE, including obtaining specific information regarding public announcements on initiated acquisitions, registering to receive a solicitation and submit proposals for specific transactions, and obtaining information and guidance on the Department’s acquisition and financial assistance award processes, you are encouraged to visit the Department’s FedConnect and FedBizOpps.gov.

DOE Acquisition Forecast Opportunities Website Disclosures:

1. It is the policy of U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) to provide timely information to the public concerning DOE's forecast of future prime and subcontracting opportunities which are available through DOE's program offices, major sites and facilities management contractors (FMCs).  This forecast has been expanded to also provide timely status information for ongoing prime contracting actions that are valued in excess of the simplified acquisition threshold.  As the largest civilian contracting agency within the Federal government, DOE believes that enhancing the forecast to provide a greater level of advance and up-to-date information pertaining to DOE's future and ongoing contracting opportunities will substantially improve the nature of and accessibility to the kind of procurement information that is important to DOE's management, the public, industry, Congress, and DOE stakeholders.
2.  Information contained in the forecast for future and ongoing transactions can be searched and sorted in a number of ways, including by:
     a. DOE's contracting activity, state, type of acquisition, and North American Industrial Classification Code (NAICS Code)
     b. In addition, points of contact are identified for each opportunity that is listed for access to additional information.  You may view and download DOE's Small Business Program Manager's Directory (SBPMs Directory)
3.  The acquisitions identified in this forecast are expected to be solicited and/or awarded in this fiscal year and beyond, based on the best information available at the time of publication.  All projected acquisitions and associated information are subject to revision or cancellation.  Final decisions regarding the extent of competition, socioeconomic considerations, estimated value, etc., will not be made until each acquisition is formally initiated.  The data and information that is contained in this forecast should be considered for informational and planning purposed only -- it does not represent a pre-solicitation synopsis, nor does it constitute an invitation for bid or request for proposal, or a commitment by the Government to purchase the described supplies and services.
4.  For specific information on doing business with DOE, including obtaining specific information regarding public announcements on initiated acquisitions, registering to receive a solicitation and submit proposals for specific transactions, and obtaining information and guidance on DOE's acquisition and financial assistance award processes, you are encouraged to visit:
     a. FedConnect
     b. FedBizOpps.gov
5.  To be considered a small business, you must:
     a. be registered in the System for Award Management (SAM) for the NAICS code associated with that opportunity
     b. meet the required Business Size Standard as established by the Small Business Administration (SBA)
6.  Major revisions or cancellations may occur with contracting opportunities.
7.  DOE reserves the right to change the competition type from Competitive to Set-Aside prior to the release of the Request for Quotation (RFQ).
8.  You may search for a DOE's contact card by viewing DOE's Phonebook.
9.  Advertising or marketing information submission is prohibited on this site.
