Swift & Staley Recognized at DOE 13th Annual Small Business Forum

September 22, 2014 - 9:45am

Addthis

Kevin Knobloch, Chief of Staff, US DOE, Swift & Staley's Diane Snow and Patricia Holsapple, and OSDBU Director John Hale III at 13th Annual Small Business Forum, June 12, 2014, Tampa, FL

Swift & Staley, Inc. of Kevil, Kentucky, won the Small Business of the Year Award at the U.S. Department of Energy’s 13th Annual Small Business Forum on June 12, 2014 in Tampa, FL. CEO Patricia Holsapple and Project Manager Diane Snow represented SST at the ceremony, where they received the award from John Hale III, Director of DOE’s Office of Small and Diverse Business Utilization (OSDBU) and Kevin Knobloch, Chief of Staff for the Department of Energy.

The Small Business of the Year Award recognizes the creative, unique and extraordinary performance of a small business which has significantly and directly impacted core DOE mission objectives and requirements. Criteria require that the recipient excels beyond expectations or contractual obligations by providing creative or unique solutions to DOE requirements and extraordinary customer service and/or significant value (related to schedule and budget).

Swift & Staley Inc. (SST) is a woman-owned small business responsible for safe, efficient execution of the Paducah Gaseous Diffusion Plant (PGDP) Infrastructure Services contract.  SST provides facility and grounds maintenance services for the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) and their contractors at the PGDP site. 

SST’s performance has helped DOE and its prime contractors achieve significant goals and objectives, including obtaining DOE Voluntary Protection Program (VPP) Merit status recognition, a first in the history of PGDP.  In addition, SST worked more than 1.3 million man-hours without a lost time injury or illness, representing over eight years of operation.

SST completed these tasks while supporting a 55% increase in commitments for DOE support activities from FY 2012, completing 98% of contract milestones and 100% of corrective actions on or ahead of schedule.

SST also developed and implemented a script to encrypt emails by default, thus helping protect users’ Personal Identifiable Information (PII).  As the first line of defense, this script prevented 216 potential cyber security incidents for 2013.  SST also created a simple, efficient script that reminds the computer user to remove their HSPD-12 identification badge from their card reader after logging into the system; this helps prevent theft of badges and accidental loss of system access credentials.  This script has been shared with DOE Headquarters in order to improve security and operational security across the federal complex.

To communicate the importance of Information Technology (IT)/cyber security and find innovative solutions for the entire PPPO complex, SST initiated and hosted the site’s first Cyber Summit.  During this activity, representatives from DOE PPPO and its contractors gathered to collaborate on upcoming projects, provide training, share working knowledge, and review lessons learned in order to improve and standardize security processes across PPPO.

SST also consolidated records, closed the Document Management Center (DMC), reduced security requirements, and saved thousands of dollars in reduction of the overall footprint.  To achieve this, SST completed a critical Performance Based Incentive to empty the DMC by May 31, 2013.  SST completed this milestone ahead of schedule by copying, transferring to electronic format, and shipping 1,622 cubic feet of hardcopy records to a Federal Records Center. 

SST achieved DOE Laboratory Accreditation Program (DOELAP) certification.  SST is the first Paducah Site contractor to receive a primary DOELAP accreditation.  SST’s approach in designing and implementing its internal and external dose monitoring programs made use of commercial, as opposed to government, laboratories and dosimetry providers, thus saving in excess of $30K.  SST now has the ability to provide dosimetry services to site tenants at greatly reduced costs.

Recognizing the need to recycle and/or reuse assets, SST seeks alternatives for excess materials and equipment.  In FY 2013, SST recycled and/or reused 883,065 pounds of materials and equipment (a 600% increase over FY 2012), including used oil, concrete, electronics, clean scrap materials, asphalt/rock, and equipment.

SST also:
• identified a reduction in the use of power for a Paducah Site project, then coordinated with another site contractor and the electrical utility to sell back this unused power, saving the government $61,354 in FY 2013.
• implemented the Energy Independence and Security Act (EISA) 432, Energy and Water Evaluation requirement on an annual basis with 100% completion.  Paducah was the only DOE Environmental Management (EM) site that exceeded requirements without additional funding.  DOE-EM used SST’s approach as its “Best Practice” for implementing the EISA 432 requirement and shared it with other EM and non-EM sites and
• developed and deployed a Hazard Tracking database that provides employees with a quick and anonymous way to submit recognized hazards in the workplace.

This is the third recognition for SST.  The DOE Office of Environmental Management’s Portsmouth/Paducah Project Office (PPPO) have worked with SST since 2004.  They were nominated and received the 2007 DOE HUBZone Small Business of the Year Award and the 2008 DOE Small Business of the Year Award.
