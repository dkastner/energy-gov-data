Wastren-EnergX Mission Support Wins 8(a)/Small Disadvantaged Business of the Year Award

September 23, 2014 - 9:40am

Addthis

Damon Detillion, Project Manager for Wastren-EnergX Mission Support, Accepts 8(a)/Small Disadvantaged Business of the Year Award from OSDBU Director John Hale, June 12, 2014, Tampa, FL

On June 12, 2014, Wastren-EnergX Mission Support, LLC (WEMS) won the 8(a)/Small Disadvantaged Business of the Year Award at the U.S. Department of Energy’s (DOE) 13th Annual Small Business Forum in Tampa, FL. Project Manager Damon Detillion represented WEMS at the ceremony, where he received the award from John Hale, Director of DOE’s Office of Small and Diverse Business Utilization (OSDBU).

The 8(a)/Small Disadvantaged Business of the Year Award recognizes the outstanding performance of a small business, certified by the U.S. Small Business Administration’s 8(a) and/or Small Disadvantaged Business programs, for their exceptional performance in activities contributing to core DOE mission accomplishments. The recipient must excel beyond expectations or contract requirements by providing creative or unique solutions to DOE requirements and extraordinary customer service, and/or significant value (schedule and budget related).

WEMS provides facility support services  at the Portsmouth Gaseous Diffusion Plant (PORTS) in Piketon, Ohio, which comprises 3,778 acres.  WEMS supports DOE’s decontamination and decommissioning  project as well as other DOE initiatives. 
WEMS’s 168 employees plan, organize, and perform 11 “performance work statement” (PWS) activities that compose the PORTS FSS prime contract. The scope of work includes:

• Security oversight (physical, personnel, information, and cyber)
• Computer and telecommunications services
• Roads, grounds, and facilities maintenance
• Records management, document control, and mail services
• Property management
• Site-access training
• Shipping and receiving

WEMS completes these activities by: taking a proactive, integrated approach to work performance; listening to customers; and working with all site entities to ensure mission success while focusing on the best value to the government.  The 2013 nomination letter describes several ways in which WEMS excelled at providing creative solutions, extraordinary customer service, and significant value during fiscal year 2013 (FY13). Here are some excerpts.

SAFETY

• WEMS continued its exceptional safety performance:
• Achieved zero “lost workday cases” (LWCs) and zero “recordable injuries”
• Re-certified as DOE Voluntary Protection Program (VPP) “star” site in June, 2013
• Received third DOE VPP “star of excellence” award in August, 2013

Additionally, WEMS’s safety representative for the United Steel Workers received DOE’s FY13 VPP contractor “champion of the year” award, for support of DOE’s VPP program.  (A WEMS employee has received the award two years in a row).  Also, the Ohio Bureau of Workers’ Compensation awarded three awards to WEMS for safety performance:

• 100% award (no lost workday cases)
• Group award (employer with the lowest incident rate) 
• Special award (500,000 hours without an LWC)
• WEMS led VPP involvement at other sites and locally:
• Actively mentored Swift and Staley (SST), Paducah’s FSS contractor, from October 2012 through May 2013 — at which time SST received VPP merit status
• Served on the VPP committee of the PORTS D&D contractor
• Participated on DOE’s VPP assessment team for Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL) in June 2013; LANL received VPP merit status

Additionally, a WEMS safety professional serves as president of the local safety council and VPP ambassador for more than 100 area businesses.

SUSTAINABILITY

WEMS completed 28 sustainability activities to receive the federal electronics challenge (FEC) “gold” award for efforts in recycling and environmentally efficient use of electronics — having previously received “bronze” and “silver” FEC awards.  Impacts at PORTS include energy savings and reduced toxic wastes, waste stream volumes, and disposal costs.

WEMS was instrumental in PORTS receiving a “silver” award under DOE’s “green buy award program,” which recognizes DOE sites that successfully purchase products with leadership-level sustainability attributes — purchases that save energy, conserve water, and reduce health and environmental impacts.  WEMS’s purchases accounted for six of the nine products required in three categories. 

Sustainability efforts include a “think before you ink” campaign (which reduced paper consumption 11%), power management control of computers and monitors, and installation of approximately 200 additional “LED” light bulbs.

The Office of Environmental Management, Portsmouth/Paducah Project Office (PPPO) has worked with WEMS since 2009 when they were awarded the contract for the Portsmouth site facility support services.  During this contract (2010-2011), WEMS has been a partner with PPPO as the site transitioned the Gaseous Diffusion Plant from Cold Shutdown to Decontamination and Decommissioning.  This transfer increased the support needed from WEMS by more than twice what it was previously supporting.
