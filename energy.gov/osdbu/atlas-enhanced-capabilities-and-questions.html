ATLAS Enhanced Capabilities and Questions

Enhanced Capabilities

ATLAS provides an enterprise wide solution for managing DOE’s transportation activities.  ATLAS combined and enhanced the existing tools in a reliable, efficient, user friendly and secure cloud platform.  ATLAS tools and electronic forms were designed by expert users to promote compliance in an intuitive (logical and easy to use) format and to provide easy access for qualified users.   Authenticated users can reliably access and use ATLAS with their desktop computer or smartphone from all DOE sites 24/7/365.   ATLAS delivers cost savings and provides new and detailed information for informed management decisions. 
 

  • ATLAS brings together transportation information and tools to create compliant and cost effective shipments for DOE.  DOE shippers and transportation administrators have access to DOE’s lowest negotiated transportation rates (tenders) and can perform queries and comparisons on the supplier data for general needs such as specific packaging, transportation equipment types, and commodities transported, or for specific carrier information such as driver types and clearances, current insurance coverage, registrations/permits, terminal locations, company contact information and other information needed to arrange shipments.
     
  • Transportation vendors have direct input into ATLAS.  Vendors including UPS, FedEx, and over 60 authorized trucking firms have their own private profile on the system.  These vendors maintain company specific information on packaging, equipment inventories, shipping rates, compliance documents, corporate contacts, etc. to make it easy for DOE shippers to have the latest and complete information to plan shipments.
     
  • Shippers can make electronic payments via ATLAS using a commercial tool provided by GSA’s TPPS (Third Party Payment System).  ATLAS provides a secure portal for electronic payments made by shippers to their vendors. Vendors appreciate the expedited payment, while shippers appreciate the reduced bureaucracy and cost of paper management. 
     
  • DOE shippers are moving to ATLAS.   ATLAS was built with expert user input to be user friendly and meet the needs of a large group of users across the DOE complex.  Users of the legacy systems are unanimously moving to ATLAS.  Shippers using unique site solutions are also moving to ATLAS.  This widespread use of ATLAS provides efficiencies and new and more detailed information for DOE management decisions.  
     
  • ATLAS delivers business intelligence (information) to enable effective management decisions.   By providing access to pertinent shipping and transportation information in a timely, targeted, and prioritized manner, ATLAS allows DOE management to make critical decisions for current needs and future initiatives.   ATLAS is a long term asset for supporting mission needs.

 

Questions

How much did it cost?
A modest investment enabled enhancements and migration to an efficient cloud platform.

The migration from ATMS to ATLAS was accomplished with only a modest investment within existing budgets.  The basic components for the new system were in place but required enhancement and integration.  Boston Government Services, LLC (BGS), DOE’s contractor, was able to update and integrate existing systems and tools for an investment equal to about 25% of the annual cost that DOE expended to operate and maintain the old system. 

The timing was perfect for DOE’s movement to a secure cloud environment.  Lockheed Martin as part of the Hanford Mission Support Alliance had developed a cloud platform that had security features and controls needed for a federal application.  BGS worked with Lockheed Martin and DOE Information Management and Security programs to qualify the cloud based platform and allow DOE to take advantage of the effectiveness of cloud computing.  DOE’s transportation data was migrated from legacy servers nearing the end of their useful life, to the cloud platform.  Simply avoiding the cost of replacing the aging servers covered most of the cost of the migration to the secure cloud platform. 

The annual cost of maintaining ATLAS on the cloud is about 75% lower than the annual cost of maintaining the old system.  Improved accessibility (cloud technology enables complex wide accessibility) and reliability (cloud technology eliminated the cost and impact of hardware failures for users) are continual bonuses.  Bottom line – for an investment of a few hundred thousand dollars, millions of dollars will be saved each year in transportation cost.  DOE’s savings from reduced maintenance and replacement costs will pay for system development and migration in about 3 years. 

Now that it’s built, will it be used?
DOE transportation activities are rapidly moving to ATLAS, the cloud based transportation and logistics analysis system.

The value of ATLAS is maximized when it is adopted by all potential users, because this maximizes the savings, and the data available for management.   The users of the old DOE transportation tools and systems are now using ATLAS.  This is about 250 users.  There are several hundred potential additional users across the DOE complex.  New users are finding updated and integrated components that are user friendly and that have dramatically enhanced the capabilities.  As potential users learn about ATLAS, they are migrating to the new system.

ATLAS was built to serve the entire DOE complex.  Meeting user needs was a primary consideration in developing ATLAS.  Soliciting user input began with simple questions “why don’t you use the existing tools?” and “what do you need from an automated transportation management solution?”  User input was vital to developing user friendly forms that complied with federal rules and regulations.  At every step of the development process user input was solicited and incorporated to improve the usefulness and ease of operation of the system.  Collaboration among transportation subject matter experts and the ATLAS system developers was essential in creating ATLAS.

Responding to user’s needs continues today as ideas, suggestions, or issues are fed to an industry group of transportation experts who provide guidance for the future enhancements of ATLAS.
