During this session, you will learn some commonsense approaches to help you map out a winning game plan to increase the odds of your company’s future success.

Marketing to Win: How to Identify the Real Decision-maker and Tailor Your Message

Speaker: Gloria Larkin, President TargetGov
Description: Every federal contract involves a team of decision-makers. Attendees of this session will learn how to identify all of the layers of decision-makers, where to find them, when to reach out and how to do so.

Finding and Winning Simplified Acquisitions Part 1

Speaker: Guy Timberlake, Chief Visionary Officer and Chief Executive Officer, The American Small Business Coalition
Description: In the course of spending hundreds of billions of dollars each fiscal year, federal agencies leverage a number of methods to purchase goods and services to support operational needs. One of the easiest and more efficient methods (by design) is known as the Simplified Acquisition Procedures or SAP. The Simplified Acquisition Procedures are used by over sixty (60) federal agencies, boards and commissions, and represents one of the few areas of government spending realizing consistent increases. This interactive two part session will provide attendees a solid background on what Simplified Acquisitions are; how they are used by various federal agencies; how much agencies spend under this purchase method; what goods and services are procured; competition; award types used; how to find contacts and opportunities related to Simplified Acquisitions using FPDS-NG and FBO.gov and much more!

Bid Decision Gate Reviews

Speaker: Tan Wilson, PMP, President of Entellect, LLC
Description: Who has unlimited amount of bid and proposal funds? No one and this includes the large government contractors. Terms such as a pipeline, blue team, black hat and other qualifying gate reviews are often spoken, but rarely executed or given the proper time and respect they deserve. If you find excuses for not qualifying your pipeline or forgoing an opportunity review, then stop immediately. You must begin to lay the foundation to adopt better business development processes and decision making activities within your organizations.  These decision gates are business development milestones where the business development team and key executives can decide whether to continue or initiate capture, or no bid and end the pursuit. Stop spending good money over bad bid and proposal efforts that won’t result in a contract win.

Proposal Preparedness

Speaker: Tan Wilson, PMP, President of Entellect, LLCDescription: Do you have a draft RFP or a previous solicitation from 5 years ago? Then what are you waiting for? Get started on simple proposal preparedness activities by getting organized and a jump start on your response before the final solicitation is released. It may seem like a waste of money, but you’ll spend less in the long run by investing time and resources now rather than later. Learn before the final is released if you have the right qualified team members; sufficient proposal resources (in-house or contract staff); or begin to collect critical metrics and proof points to support your past performance and experience. Prevent and minimize the stress and unforeseen proposal expenses by establishing a foundation for a well-organized and disciplined proposal response process.

The Power of Effective Networking

Speaker: William Osgood, President and CEO, CFR & Associates
Description: This is one of the more exciting workshops because very few of us are gifted in the area of networking. In this workshop, we will examine the do’s and don’ts of effective networking. We will evaluate, plan, and prepare a powerful 30 second “Elevator” pitch, a powerful and effective 10 minute briefing and look at what is necessary for effective longer presentations. We will evaluate and critique effective trade show and expo marketing strategies. Each attendee should leave with some skills and strategies learned about networking that will help them with the ultimate objective of developing those skills to build Win/Win/Win relationships.
